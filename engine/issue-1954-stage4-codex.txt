## Headline
WFH for Civil Servants Is Not Flexibility — It Is Fuel Rationing

## Context
The government announced phased work-from-home arrangements for non-critical civil servants, framed as modern flexibility. The actual trigger is fuel conservation during the Hormuz crisis. Frontline services — healthcare, education, enforcement — are excluded. States are scrambling to follow without federal guidelines. Decision expected after Hari Raya.

## Source Date
2026-03-30

## Key Facts
- PM Anwar announced WFH to be rolled out "gradually and selectively" on 26 March 2026
- Announced same day as RON95 quota cut and Hari Raya open house cancellation
- Framed as part of fuel conservation measures amid West Asia crisis
- Frontline services excluded: healthcare, education, enforcement
- Non-critical administrative roles eligible
- Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil: final decision expected after Hari Raya
- No implementation details published: who qualifies, how many days, what infrastructure

- Private sector "urged" to follow suit but no mandate
- Malaysia has ~1.7 million civil servants (approximate, commonly cited figure)
- During COVID, WFH was implemented but rolled back quickly post-pandemic
- No mention of productivity targets, performance metrics, or remote work infrastructure investment

- Building operational costs (electricity, maintenance) for government offices not addressed
