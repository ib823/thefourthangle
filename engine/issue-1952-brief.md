# ISSUE BRIEF: 1952

## Headline
Fuel Quota Cut Hits April 1 — Who Actually Loses

## Context
The monthly subsidised RON95 quota under BUDI95 drops from 300 litres to 200 litres effective 1 April 2026. The government says 90% of users consume under 200 litres. The subsidy bill has reached RM4 billion per month with crude above $110/barrel. E-hailing drivers retain 800L quota. Market price for RON95 above quota is RM3.35/litre vs subsidised RM1.99/litre.

## Source Date
2026-03-30

## Key Facts
- BUDI95 quota cut from 300L to 200L per month, effective 1 April 2026
- Subsidised price stays at RM1.99/litre; market price ~RM3.35/litre (68% more)
- PM Anwar says 90% of RON95 users consume under 200L/month
- E-hailing/gig workers retain 800L/month quota
- Industry group says 800L still insufficient for high-mileage drivers (4,000-5,000km/month)
- Monthly subsidy bill reached RM4 billion (crude above $100/barrel)
- Annual bill could hit RM24 billion if crude stays above $110/barrel
- Malaysia is simultaneously a net oil exporter and net refined fuel importer
- Temporary measure "until global energy markets stabilise"
- Long-distance rural commuters, delivery riders, plantation workers most affected
- East Malaysia has limited public transport alternatives
- No timeline given for when/if quota returns to 300L
- Diesel also facing temporary limits in East Malaysia
- Hari Raya price controls on essentials ended 28 March
