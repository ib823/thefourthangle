ISSUE: One Death, Six Proposals, Zero Data — Malaysia's Drunk Driving Blind Spot
PERIOD: 29 March 2026 – ongoing

CONTEXT:
- On 29 March 2026 at 11:40am in Klang, a 20-year-old male driving a Honda City — positive for both alcohol and drugs — crossed into oncoming traffic on Jalan Raya Barat and struck motorcyclist Amirul Hafiz Omar head-on. Omar died at the scene.
- Omar was 34, a warehouse worker by day and Lalamove delivery rider by night, father of three children aged 2-9. He had left home to buy a maths book for his eldest child.
- The driver was remanded for four days. Case investigated under Section 44(1) Road Transport Act 1987 and Section 15(1)(a) Dangerous Drugs Act 1952.
- Within 24 hours, at least four opposition MPs called for capital punishment for drunk drivers causing death. Machang MP Wan Ahmad Fayhsal led the call.
- Transport Minister Anthony Loke announced amendments to Act 333 for mandatory compensation from convicted DUI offenders to victims' families.
- Religious Affairs Minister Zulkifli Hasan proposed diyat (Islamic blood money) as a mechanism for victim families.
- The incident spiralled into ethnic race-baiting on social media (SCMP reported explicitly), though public sentiment largely pushed back for policy focus.

RELEVANT LAW:
- Road Transport Act 1987 (Act 333), amended by Road Transport (Amendment) Act 2020 (effective 23 Oct 2020)
- BAC limit: 50mg per 100ml blood (reduced from 80mg), 22 micrograms per 100ml breath (reduced from 35), 67mg per 100ml urine
- DUI causing death (first offence): 10-15 years prison, RM50,000-100,000 fine, minimum 10-year licence ban
- DUI causing death (repeat): 15-20 years prison, RM100,000-150,000 fine
- Dangerous Drugs Act 1952 Section 15(1)(a) — driving under influence of drugs
- Gig Workers Bill 2025 — mandates SOCSO registration by platform operators

KEY STATISTICS:
- 149 DUI cases charged in court Jan 2021 – Sep 2024 (Home Affairs Minister, Nov 2024)
- 1,261 convicted of driving under influence in 2025 (Transport Minister)
- DUI fatalities: 0.2% of fatal crashes (12-13 deaths/year from 6,400+ fatal crashes)
- Malaysia does NOT mandate post-crash alcohol testing for all fatal crash drivers
- Australia mandates post-crash blood testing; alcohol involvement found in 25-30% of fatal crashes
- Motorcyclists: 59% of road fatalities (MIROS); recent data shows 66.4% in 2025
- Only 3% of road infrastructure rated 3-star+ for motorcycle safety (iRAP/ATO)
- ~6,000+ road deaths per year (~17 per day)
- Road crashes cost ~RM25.6 billion annually (~4% of GDP)
- WHO fatality rate: 13.9 per 100,000 population (2021)
- 1.2+ million gig workers in Malaysia
- Malaysia abolished mandatory death penalty in 2023
