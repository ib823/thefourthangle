# ISSUE 1962 — Two Generals Charged: Malaysia's First Military Corruption Prosecution

## PERIOD
December 2025–April 2026

## CONTEXT (Timeline)
- Dec 2025: MACC launches probe into military procurement contracts 2023–2025
- Jan 2026: Former Army Chief Gen Hafizuddiean Jantan and wife arrested
- Jan 21, 2026: AG consents to charge Hafizuddiean and former Defence Forces Chief Gen Mohamad Nizam Jaafar
- Jan 23, 2026: Second general (Nizam) formally charged — abuse of power, criminal breach of trust
- Jan 2026: MACC seizes ~$13 million (RM58M) in cash, gold, luxury goods from raids
- Jan 2026: 80+ bank accounts frozen, 23 military staff and civilians arrested
- Jan 2026: King (Yang di-Pertuan Agong) warns of wider corruption "hunt" in military
- Jan 28, 2026: Reporting confirms suspected 26-firm cartel bribed senior officers
- Ongoing: 158 projects exceeding RM500K and 4,500+ smaller projects under investigation

## ACTORS
- Gen (Rtd) Hafizuddiean Jantan — Former Army Chief (Sep 2023–late 2025), charged under money laundering
- Salwani Anuar — Wife of Hafizuddiean, co-charged
- Gen (Rtd) Mohamad Nizam Jaafar — Former Defence Forces Chief (Jan–Dec 2025), charged with abuse of power and CBT
- MACC — Investigating body
- Attorney General — Granted consent to prosecute
- Yang di-Pertuan Agong — Publicly warned of wider military corruption hunt
- 26-firm cartel — Suspected network of contractors bribing officers for procurement contracts
- Ministry of Defence — Oversight of procurement system that enabled corruption

## RELEVANT LAW
- Anti-Money Laundering, Anti-Terrorism Financing and Proceeds of Unlawful Activities Act (AMLA) 2001
- MACC Act 2009, Section 23 — Abuse of position/power
- Penal Code Section 409 — Criminal breach of trust by public servant
- Armed Forces Act 1972 — Military discipline and procurement rules
- Federal Constitution Article 132 — Public services including armed forces

## KEY STATISTICS
- 2 — Former military chiefs charged (first time in Malaysian history)
- $13 million (~RM58M) — Cash, gold, luxury goods seized
- 80+ — Bank accounts frozen
- 23 — Military staff and civilians arrested
- 26 — Firms in suspected bribery cartel
- 158 — Projects exceeding RM500K under investigation
- 4,500+ — Smaller projects also under scrutiny
- RM6.1B — Already wasted on zero-delivery LCS programme (separate but related defence procurement failure)
