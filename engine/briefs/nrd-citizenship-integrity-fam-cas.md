# ISSUE 1976 — When Forged Ancestry Got Past the NRD: Malaysia's Citizenship Verification on Trial

## PERIOD
2024 – April 2026

## CONTEXT (Timeline)

- **1957**: Births and Deaths Registration Act 1957 (Act 299) enacted; sets the framework for birth registration and the documentary basis for citizenship by descent claims
- **1959**: National Registration Act 1959 (Act 78) and the National Registration Regulations 1990 — define the legal authority of the National Registration Department (Jabatan Pendaftaran Negara, NRD) to issue identity and citizenship documents
- **2024 (early)**: FAM begins approaching foreign-born players — Facundo Tomás Garcés Rattaro, Rodrigo Julián Holgado, Imanol Javier Machuca, João Vítor Brandão Figueiredo, Gabriel Felipe Arrocha, Jon Irazabal Iraurgui, Hector Alejandro Hevel Serrano — advising they might lawfully seek Malaysian citizenship via documented ancestral links and play for the Harimau Malaya
- **2024–2025**: Citizenship documents (including birth certificates establishing supposed Malaysian-born grandparents) are processed and issued by NRD; players receive Malaysian passports and become eligible to represent Malaysia at FIFA level
- **2025 Jun 10**: All seven players debut for Malaysia in 2027 AFC Asian Cup qualifiers, including a 4–0 win over Vietnam at Bukit Jalil
- **2025 Sep 26**: FIFA Disciplinary Committee fines FAM CHF 350,000 (~RM1.9 million); imposes 12-month bans on all 7 players from all football-related activities; finds documents submitted by FAM contained forged signatures and altered details
- **2025 Nov 3**: FIFA Appeal Committee rejects FAM's appeal; full reasoned decision later published, exposing the documentary forgery trail
- **2025 Nov 19**: FIFA opens further investigation into FAM's conduct in the document submission process
- **2025 Nov–Dec**: Domestic political pressure mounts; questions raised in Parliament about the role of NRD and Home Ministry in issuing citizenship to applicants whose ancestry claims FIFA could disprove with publicly available foreign records
- **2026 Mar 5**: Court of Arbitration for Sport (CAS) issues its decision: dismisses FAM's consolidated appeal in full and **upholds the CHF 350,000 fine on FAM**; partially upholds the players' appeal by limiting the 12-month ban to *official matches only* (not training/all activities); CAS issues media release №11990
- **2026 Mar 5**: AFC officially "takes note" of CAS decision and refers matter to its own Disciplinary and Ethics Committee
- **2026 Mar 17**: AFC Disciplinary and Ethics Committee overturns Malaysia's two 2027 AFC Asian Cup qualifying match results — vs Vietnam and vs Nepal — to 0–3 forfeits
- **2026 Mar–Apr**: Renewed civil society and parliamentary calls for an independent inquiry into how NRD verified the underlying ancestry documents; activists draw the contrast with statelessness cases where Malaysian-born children of irregular marriages cannot get NRD recognition under Article 14
- **2026 Apr (ongoing)**: Home Minister Saifuddin Nasution Ismail under pressure to disclose the NRD verification trail; no public report released as of early April 2026

## ACTORS

- **National Registration Department (NRD / Jabatan Pendaftaran Negara, JPN)** — Federal agency under the Ministry of Home Affairs; legal authority to issue birth certificates and citizenship documents; the institutional locus of the verification failure
- **Ministry of Home Affairs (KDN)** — Parent ministry of NRD; Home Minister Saifuddin Nasution Ismail
- **Football Association of Malaysia (FAM)** — Submitted the documents to FIFA; fined CHF 350,000 by FIFA, sanction upheld by CAS
- **FIFA Disciplinary Committee** — Made original finding that documents were forged
- **Court of Arbitration for Sport (CAS), Lausanne** — Issued 5 March 2026 decision dismissing FAM's appeal
- **Asian Football Confederation (AFC) Disciplinary & Ethics Committee** — On 17 March 2026 forfeited Malaysia's qualifying match results
- **The seven players** — Garcés, Holgado, Machuca, Figueiredo, Arrocha, Irazabal, Hevel; CAS limited their bans to official matches only
- **DHRRA Malaysia (Development of Human Resources for Rural Areas)** — NGO that has documented at least 16,392 stateless persons in Peninsular Malaysia (2016 – June 2023); the structural counter-image to the FAM case
- **SUHAKAM (Human Rights Commission of Malaysia)** — Has repeatedly flagged statelessness as a documentation/NRD-process issue
- **C4 Center, Bersih, G25** — Civil society voices calling for institutional accountability beyond FAM

## RELEVANT LAW

- **Federal Constitution, Article 14** — Acquisition of citizenship by operation of law; Article 14(1)(b) read with Part II of the Second Schedule defines citizenship by descent and by birth in Malaysia
- **Federal Constitution, Article 15** — Citizenship by registration of wives and children
- **Federal Constitution, Article 16** — Citizenship by registration of persons born in the Federation before Merdeka Day
- **Federal Constitution, Article 17 (repealed)** — Historical provision; cited here for context on registration pathways
- **Federal Constitution, Article 19** — Citizenship by naturalisation; the discretionary federal power being scrutinised
- **Births and Deaths Registration Act 1957 (Act 299)** — Statutory framework for birth registration; the document type at the centre of the FIFA forgery finding
- **National Registration Act 1959 (Act 78)** and **National Registration Regulations 1990** — Authority and procedures for NRD identity-document issuance
- **Adoption Act 1952 / Registration of Adoptions Act 1952** — Relevant to the wider documentation/statelessness context
- **FIFA Statutes, Article 7** and **FIFA Regulations Governing the Application of the Statutes, Article 5–8** — Eligibility rules requiring "clear connection" to a country (e.g., birth, parent or grandparent born there); the rules that exposed the discrepancy
- **Penal Code, Sections 463–471** — Forgery offences; potential domestic exposure for any individual proven to have forged the underlying documents

## KEY STATISTICS

- **7** — Players sanctioned by FIFA for forged ancestry documents
- **CHF 350,000 (~RM1.9 million)** — FAM fine upheld by CAS on 5 March 2026
- **CHF 2,000 (~RM10,560)** — Individual fine on each of the 7 players
- **12 months** — Length of player bans (initially "all activities," later limited to "official matches only" by CAS)
- **2** — Match results overturned by AFC on 17 March 2026 (vs Vietnam, vs Nepal) to 0–3 forfeits
- **16,392** — Stateless persons documented by DHRRA in Peninsular Malaysia between 2016 – June 2023
- **9,392** — Of those, still in statelessness limbo as of mid-2023
- **7,000** — Recognised as Malaysians through DHRRA accompaniment efforts (the slow, case-by-case route)
- **50,000–100,000** — Commonly cited civil society estimate of total stateless persons in Malaysia (T4A's own issue 0179)
- **3** — DHRRA's identified causes of statelessness: marriage irregularity, NRD inconsistency district-to-district, and unreformed law
- **1** — Reported time period (years) within which the seven naturalised players reportedly received their Malaysian documentation (versus the multi-year/decade waits faced by stateless applicants)

3. **Ethnic** — MEDIUM: Touches sensitive citizenship/ancestry questions; T4A must frame as institutional process critique only, never as commentary on the players' identities or any community
4. **Religious** — LOW: Not a religious matter

6. **Completeness** — CRITICAL: Coverage is overwhelmingly sports-section; the institutional NRD/Home Ministry verification angle remains under-reported
7. **Temporal** — HIGH: CAS decision (5 Mar), AFC penalty (17 Mar), ongoing pressure through April 2026
8. **Confidence** — HIGH: CAS media release №11990 is public; FIFA Disciplinary decision public; cross-confirmed across The Star, FMT, Malay Mail, NST, ESPN, SCMP, Daily Sabah, Wikipedia
9. **Sources** — HIGH: 18 sources spanning international wire (ESPN, SCMP), national press, sports specialist outlets, legal commentary (Conventus Law, Richard Wee Chambers), and human rights NGOs (DHRRA, SUHAKAM)
10. **Geographic** — MEDIUM: Federal NRD; international (Lausanne CAS, Zurich FIFA, Kuala Lumpur AFC); diaspora-citizenship questions
11. **Economic** — MEDIUM: Sports investment, sponsorship, broadcasting fallout; reputational cost on Malaysian institutional credibility
12. **Gender** — MEDIUM: Citizenship gender-discrimination is the dominant pathway by which Malaysian-born children become stateless; relevant comparator throughout

## RECOMMENDED LENSES

**Governance** — The institutional question: how did NRD verify the foreign birth certificates that FIFA later proved were forged? Was any cross-check done against foreign civil registries? What is the standard verification protocol for ancestry-based citizenship claims under Article 14?

**Legal** — Article 14 read with Article 19, the Births and Deaths Registration Act 1957, and the FIFA eligibility rules in Article 7 of the FIFA Statutes — three legal regimes that intersected and exposed each other.

**Critical** — The hardest editorial frame: Malaysia's citizenship apparatus appears to have acted *quickly* for connected foreign athletes while moving *slowly or not at all* for the 16,000+ documented stateless persons born on Malaysian soil. The legal regime is the same Article 14. The disparity is the story.
