# ISSUE 1978 — When the PM's Aide Was Charged: The Sabah Mining Licence Pipeline on Trial

## PERIOD
November 2023 – April 2026

## CONTEXT (Timeline)

- **1960**: Sabah Mining Ordinance (SMO) enacted; under Section 6, the Chief Minister alone holds the legal authority to grant mining or prospecting licences in Sabah
- **1994**: Federal Mineral Development Act (Act 525) passed, but Sabah retains licensing authority under its own ordinance
- **1999**: Sabah Mineral Enactment passed by state assembly — but never brought into force; SMO 1960 remains the governing statute
- **2023 Nov 24**: Shamsul Iskandar Mohd Akin allegedly agrees to accept RM100,000 cash bribe from businessman Albert Tei Jiann Cheing at a hotel car park on Jalan Sultan Hishamuddin, KL, as inducement to assist Tei-linked companies in obtaining Sabah mineral exploration licences
- **2023 Nov 28 – 2024 Sept 23**: Shamsul allegedly receives RM62,924 in deposits to a bank account used to pay rent on a Kuala Lumpur house
- **2024 Jan 29**: Shamsul allegedly obtains a further RM40,000 cash bribe from Tei for the same purpose
- **2024 (period)**: Two additional alleged bribes in the form of furniture (RM14,580.03) and electrical items (RM22,249) — same inducement pattern
- **2024 Dec**: Albert Tei begins releasing video clips through Malaysiakini implicating more than a dozen Sabah state assemblymen and senior figures in bribes-for-licences allegations; the so-called Sabah mining scandal explodes nationally
- **2025 Apr 10**: MACC chief Azam Baki publicly states Sabah CM Hajiji Noor "is not involved" in the mining licence case; FMT reports Sabah govt "never interfered" in probe
- **2025 Jul**: Tei is charged alongside Sabah assemblymen Yusof Yacob and Andi Muhammad Suryady Bandy with receiving and giving bribes totalling RM350,000 in connection with mining permits — the first criminal charges in the wider scandal
- **2025 Sep 23**: Whistleblower (Tei associate) reportedly barred from entering Sabah to attend trial — opposition criticism of state govt
- **2025 Nov 25**: Shamsul Iskandar resigns as Anwar Ibrahim's senior political secretary, hours before bribery allegations surface publicly
- **2025 Nov 25**: A second video clip surfaces in which a woman linked to Shamsul reportedly names PM Anwar as "mastermind" of the Sabah scandal videos; PM's office denies
- **2025 Dec 4**: Shamsul Iskandar @ Yusre Mohd Akin, 50, charged at KL Sessions Court before Judge Suzana Hussin with 4 counts under Section 17(a) MACC Act 2009 totalling RM176,829.03; pleads not guilty; same day Albert Tei charged with corresponding 4 counts under Section 17(b)
- **2025 Dec 5**: Fifth charge added against Shamsul — receiving RM64,924 in rental payments from Tei via bank deposits, Nov 28, 2023 to Sep 23, 2024; pleads not guilty; total charges now five totalling RM241,753.03
- **2025 Dec 5**: Albert Tei hit with corresponding fifth charge for the RM64,924; granted bail of RM70,000 with two sureties, monthly MACC reporting, no witness contact
- **2026 Feb 16**: Shamsul Iskandar applies to the Sessions Court to transfer his five graft charges to the High Court; transfer hearing fixed for April 9, 2026
- **2026 Mar 11**: At KL Sessions Court, Deputy Public Prosecutor confirms MACC's investigation can continue *despite* charges already being filed — prosecution states there is no law preventing concurrent investigation
- **2026 Mar 27**: Sabah elected representatives publicly press MACC for an update on the wider Sabah mining scandal probe after four months of public silence; questions raised about scope and pace
- **2026 Apr 9**: Scheduled hearing at KL High Court on Shamsul's application to transfer the graft trial up

## ACTORS

- **Datuk Seri Shamsul Iskandar @ Yusre Mohd Akin** — Former senior political secretary to PM Anwar Ibrahim, 50; charged with 5 counts under MACC Act Section 17(a); pleaded not guilty; resigned Nov 25, 2025
- **Albert Tei Jiann Cheing** — Businessman, 37, alleged briber; charged with corresponding 5 counts under Section 17(b); granted RM70,000 bail; the same individual whose video clips triggered the wider Sabah mining scandal in Dec 2024
- **PM Anwar Ibrahim** — Sitting Prime Minister; named in second video clip allegations as "mastermind" of Sabah scandal videos; office denies; political fallout central to story
- **Datuk Seri Hajiji Noor** — Sabah Chief Minister; under Section 6 of the Sabah Mining Ordinance 1960 holds sole legal authority to grant mining/prospecting licences; MACC chief publicly cleared him in April 2025
- **Yusof Yacob** — Sabah state assemblyman charged July 2025 alongside Tei and Andi Suryady in earlier RM350,000 mining permit bribes case
- **Andi Muhammad Suryady Bandy** — Sabah state assemblyman, co-accused in July 2025 charges
- **Sabah Mineral Management Sdn Bhd (SMM)** — State-owned coordination agency; explicitly NOT a licensing authority; the licensing power remains with the Chief Minister under SMO 1960
- **Judge Suzana Hussin** — Presiding KL Sessions Court judge for the Dec 2025 charges
- **MACC (Suruhanjaya Pencegahan Rasuah Malaysia)** — Investigating agency; chief Azam Baki has publicly defended pace of probe
- **Attorney General's Chambers / Deputy Public Prosecutor** — Confirms continuing investigation despite charges

## RELEVANT LAW

- **MACC Act 2009 (Act 694), Section 17(a)** — Offence by an agent of corruptly accepting gratification; max penalty 20 years imprisonment + fine of not less than 5x value of gratification or RM10,000, whichever higher; the framing for all 5 charges against Shamsul
- **MACC Act 2009 (Act 694), Section 17(b)** — Corresponding "giving" offence; framing for the 5 charges against Albert Tei
- **Sabah Mining Ordinance 1960 (Cap 109), Section 6** — Vests sole legal authority to grant mining/prospecting licences in the Chief Minister of Sabah; the structural pivot of the entire scandal
- **Sabah Mineral Enactment 1999** — Passed by Sabah State Assembly to modernise mineral tenement law; **never brought into force** — leaving Sabah's mining sector governed by 1960-era legislation
- **Mineral Development Act 1994 (Act 525)** — Federal statute regulating mineral exploration and exploitation, but explicitly excludes states (like Sabah and Sarawak) that retain their own mineral laws under MA63
- **Federal Constitution, Ninth Schedule, State List, Item 2(c)** — Reserves mines and minerals to state authority (relevant to Sabah's autonomy in licensing)
- **Whistleblower Protection Act 2010** — Relevant to questions raised about Tei's status (whistleblower vs accused) and treatment of the associate barred from entering Sabah

## RECOMMENDED LENSES

**Governance** — One legal person (the Chief Minister) holds sole licensing authority under a 65-year-old ordinance; the modernisation enacted in 1999 has never been brought into force. This is the structural story.

**Legal** — The MACC Act §17(a)/(b) symmetry, the bail conditions, the transfer-up application, and the unusual prosecution statement that investigation can continue post-charge — together form a textbook of mid-stage Malaysian graft prosecution.

**Critical** — The hardest editorial question: is prosecuting the PM's own aide *evidence the anti-graft system works* or *evidence it only works selectively*? Both readings are defensible; the considered view must reflect both.
