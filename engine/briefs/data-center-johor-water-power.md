# ISSUE 1963 — Data Centers Drink Johor Dry: First Protest, Then a Moratorium

## PERIOD
January–April 2026

## CONTEXT (Timeline)
- 2024: Malaysia emerges as major ASEAN data center hub, challenging Singapore's regional dominance with cheaper land and power
- 2024–2025: Global hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, ByteDance, GDS) announce billions in Malaysian data center investments, concentrated in Johor and Selangor
- Jul 2025: Sunway Construction Group employee investigated by MACC over alleged corruption involving RM180 million Johor data center contract
- Late 2025: TNB discloses it has received data center electricity applications exceeding 11,000MW — equivalent to 40% of Peninsular Malaysia's entire installed capacity
- Jan 2026: Johor state investment review committee begins quietly rejecting new data center applications that require liquid cooling, citing water supply constraints
- Feb 7, 2026: Malaysia's first-ever data center protest — more than 50 residents in Gelang Patah, Johor rally against Zdata Technologies facility over pollution, water depletion, and noise concerns
- Feb 2026: Zdata Technologies (Chinese-linked developer) responds to protests, promises full environmental compliance and community engagement
- Feb 2026: Johor Assemblyman publicly states the Gelang Patah dispute should serve as a "lesson" for future data center planning
- Feb 25, 2026: Federal government announces moratorium on non-AI data centers, citing unsustainable energy and water demands
- Mar 2026: PM Anwar Ibrahim confirms restrictions expected to last approximately 2 years; data centers focused on artificial intelligence workloads will receive expedited approvals
- Mar 2026: Sahabat Alam Malaysia (Friends of the Earth Malaysia) publishes analysis warning that data centers are "big energy and water guzzlers" with insufficient environmental oversight
- Ongoing: Projected data center electricity demand of 5,000–6,000MW by 2035; demand increase of 59 TWh by 2030 — equivalent to the Philippines' entire annual electricity consumption

## ACTORS

## RELEVANT LAW
- **Environmental Quality Act 1974** — EIA requirements for large-scale developments; question of whether data centers consistently trigger mandatory assessments
- **Electricity Supply Act 1990** — TNB's obligations as monopoly supplier; capacity planning and connection approvals
- **Water Services Industry Act 2006 (WSIA)** — Regulation of water supply and sewerage; SPAN's enforcement powers
- **National Water Services Commission Act 2006** — Establishes SPAN's regulatory oversight of water operators
- **Environmental Quality (Prescribed Activities) Order** — Determines which developments require EIA; data centers may fall outside mandatory categories depending on size
- **Johor state land laws** — State-level land conversion and zoning approvals for industrial development
- **Federal Constitution Article 76(1)(b)** — Federal-state division of powers over water (state matter) vs electricity (concurrent list)
- **Town and Country Planning Act 1976** — Local authority zoning and development control
