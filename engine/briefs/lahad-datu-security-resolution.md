ISSUE: Lahad Datu Anniversary Marks 13 Years Without Permanent Security Resolution — ESSCOM Still Temporary, Forward Base 41% Complete, Maritime Coverage Gaps Persist
PERIOD: March 2026 (13th anniversary of Lahad Datu incursion). Historical roots: February–March 2013.

CONTEXT:
- 11 February 2013: 235 armed militants arrived in Lahad Datu by boat. Self-proclaimed "Royal Security Forces of the Sultanate of Sulu and North Borneo," sent by Jamalul Kiram III, a claimant to the throne of the Sultanate of Sulu.
- 24 March 2013: Standoff ended after Malaysian security forces launched Operation Daulat. 72 people killed — 56 militants, 10 Malaysian security force personnel, 6 civilians.
- 7 March 2013: PM Najib Razak announced creation of ESSCOM (Eastern Sabah Security Command) to oversee the Eastern Sabah Security Zone (ESSZone).
- ESSZone covers 10 districts: Kudat, Kota Marudu, Pitas, Beluran, Sandakan, Kinabatangan, Lahad Datu, Kunak, Semporna, and Tawau — spanning 1,400 km of coastline.
- ESSCOM coordinates Royal Malaysia Police (PDRM), Malaysian Armed Forces (ATM), and Malaysian Maritime Enforcement Agency (APMM).
- 13 years later (2026): ESSCOM still operates without permanent legislation. It was established by executive order, not an Act of Parliament. Its mandate is renewed periodically.
- ESSCOM's proposed permanent security zone legislation has been deferred through multiple parliamentary sessions.

ACTORS:
- ESSCOM (Eastern Sabah Security Command) — coordinating security body for ESSZone
- Jamalul Kiram III — self-proclaimed Sultan of Sulu, ordered 2013 incursion (deceased 2013)
- Najib Razak — PM who established ESSCOM (March 2013)
- Home Minister Saifuddin Nasution Ismail — current minister overseeing ESSCOM (2025-26)
- Auditor-General's Department — flagged Pulau Mabul base delays (October 2025)
- Abu Sayyaf Group (ASG) — primary kidnap-for-ransom threat in eastern Sabah waters
- MMEA/APMM — Malaysian Maritime Enforcement Agency, partner in ESSZone operations
- Royal Malaysian Navy — Semporna naval base revamp planned
- CSIS Asia Maritime Transparency Initiative — published analysis on Malaysia's sea basing shift (May 2025)

RELEVANT LAW:
- No permanent ESSCOM Act — established by executive directive, not legislation
- National Security Council Act 2016 (Act 776) — provides some framework but ESSCOM predates it
- Security Offences (Special Measures) Act 2012 (SOSMA) — used for security detainees in Sabah
- Eastern Sabah Security Zone nighttime maritime curfew — in effect continuously since 2013
- 11th Malaysia Plan (2016-2020) — approved FOB Pulau Mabul project
- 13th Malaysia Plan (2026-2030) — ESSCOM requested RM42.93 million for 8 security projects
