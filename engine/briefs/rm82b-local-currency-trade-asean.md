# ISSUE 1972 — RM82 Billion in Local Currency Trade Sounds Like De-Dollarization. It's Mostly Re-Yuanization.

## PERIOD
2009–April 2026

## CONTEXT (Timeline)
- 1992-94: BNM forex scandal — RM31.5B losses from aggressive currency trading
- Sep 1998: Mahathir imposes capital controls, pegs ringgit at RM3.80/USD, bans offshore ringgit trading
- Jul 2005: Ringgit peg removed; managed float introduced
- Feb 2009: BNM-PBOC bilateral currency swap established (first iteration)
- 2010: Direct renminbi-ringgit trading initiated
- Jan 2015: Bank of China (Malaysia) appointed as RMB Clearing Bank in KL
- Mar 2016: Ringgit-baht LCSF launched — Malaysia's first local currency settlement framework
- Dec 2017: BNM establishes ringgit-rupiah LCSF with Bank Indonesia
- Jan 2018: All three LCSF frameworks (ringgit-rupiah, rupiah-baht, ringgit-baht) go live
- Apr 2023: Anwar tells parliament "There is no reason for Malaysia to continue depending on the dollar." Proposes Asian Monetary Fund
- Jun 2024: Anwar declares Malaysia will seek BRICS membership
- Feb 2024: Ringgit hits 26-year low of 4.7965/USD
- Oct 2024: Malaysia designated BRICS "partner country" (not full member)
- Nov 2023: BNM-PBOC swap renewed — CNY180B/RM110B, five-year term
- Feb 17, 2025: Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand harmonize and broaden LCTF — add portfolio investments, appoint new ACCDs
- May 2025: 46th ASEAN Summit in KL — ASEAN Economic Community Strategic Plan 2026-2030 pledges local currency promotion
- Jul 2025: Anwar at BRICS Rio Summit urges businesses to see BRICS as "new frontier." Trump threatens 100% tariffs on countries reducing USD role
- Feb 7-8, 2026: Modi visits Malaysia — 11 pacts signed, fast-track ringgit-rupee settlement agreed
- Q1 2025: Cross-border RMB transactions Malaysia-China surge 27% to 102 billion yuan (RM59B) — record
- Mar 24, 2026: Anwar reveals RM82.1B cumulative local currency settlements in written parliamentary reply to Lim Guan Eng

## ACTORS

- **Lim Guan Eng** — Bagan MP (DAP); asked parliamentary question about SWIFT alternatives

- **Bank of Thailand** — LCSF counterparty since March 2016
- **Bank Indonesia** — LCSF counterparty since December 2017
- **Reserve Bank of India** — new partner for ringgit-rupee settlement (Feb 2026)
- **Bank of China (Malaysia)** — RMB Clearing Bank since Jan 2015

- **Liew Chee Yoong** — UCSI University, warned de-dollarization "will falter" without simplification

## KEY STATISTICS
- **RM82.1B** — cumulative local currency settlements (2009–Nov 2025)
- China: RM62.6B (76.2%) — from 1.2% to 25.6% of bilateral trade
- Thailand: RM10B (12.2%) — from 6.4% to 17.2%
- Indonesia: RM9.5B (11.6%) — from 4.5% to 15.8%
- 2009 baseline: RM3.6B total → 22.8x growth
- Q1 2025 cross-border RMB: 102B yuan (RM59B), +27% y-o-y record
- BNM-PBOC swap: CNY180B/RM110B
- Malaysia-China trade 2024: USD212B (historic high)
- Malaysia total trade 2024: RM2.879 trillion
- ASEAN intra-regional LCS share: ~15% (from ~7% in five years)
- USD share of global trade invoicing: ~82%
- SME transaction cost savings from LCS: up to 2%
- Ringgit 26-year low: 4.7965/USD (Feb 2024)
- BNM 1990s forex losses: RM31.5B
