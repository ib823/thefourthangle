# ISSUE 1974 — DOE Leadership Corruption: The Environmental Regulator Was for Sale

## PERIOD
January 2025 – April 2026

## CONTEXT (Timeline)

- **2018 Oct 19**: Bukit Kukus paired road construction site landslide kills 9 workers in Penang; Geo Valley Sdn Bhd taken to court; project delays ensue
- **2019 Mar 7**: Kim Kim River toxic pollution in Pasir Gudang, Johor — 2.43 tonnes of hazardous chemical waste illegally dumped, 6,000 people affected, 2,775 hospitalised; exposed DOE enforcement gaps
- **2022 Apr 18 – 2024 Jul 19**: Period during which DOE Deputy DG Dr. Norhazni Mat Sari allegedly received RM21,000 in six tranches from E-Concern (Borneo) Sdn Bhd director Tang Siong Hee through her daughter's bank account
- **2022 May 25**: Norhazni allegedly received RM9,110 for home renovations from Tang Siong Hee via transfer to construction company account
- **2023 Nov 30**: DOE Director-General publicly states the department needs 3,000 additional enforcement personnel on top of existing 1,100 to monitor nearly 60,000 factories and workshops
- **2024 Jul**: Environmental Quality (Amendment) Act 2024 comes into effect with enhanced penalties — mandatory imprisonment up to 5 years for river pollution, minimum fines of RM50,000–RM100,000
- **2024 Nov 22**: Geo Valley Sdn Bhd submits letter applying for planning permission for the Bukit Kukus paired road mitigation earthworks — the project at the centre of the bribery charge
- **2025 Jan 15**: DOE Director-General Wan Abdul Latiff Wan Jaffar allegedly receives RM100,000 cash bribe from Sunrich Conquest Sdn Bhd director Khor Chong Hai (through project manager Lee Ham Kong) at DOE office in Wisma Sumber Asli, Putrajaya, as inducement to consider EIA exemption appeal for the Bukit Kukus paired road project
- **2025 Nov 1**: Sungai Johor sand washing pond failure — turbidity spikes to 37,400 NTU (normal: ~400), nearly 800,000 people lose water supply across Johor Bahru, Kota Tinggi, Kulai and Pontian; four water treatment plants shut down
- **2026 Jan 22**: MACC detains six company owners suspected of involvement in e-waste smuggling over past five years; freezes RM7.2 million in accounts
- **2026 Jan 23**: Soon Kein Chong, 40, pleads not guilty to giving a DOE officer a RM50,000 bribe to prevent action against an e-waste facility
- **2026 Jan 29**: Chinese national Jiang Fanjun, 43, pleads not guilty to conspiring to give a DOE officer RM50,000 bribe related to e-waste
- **2026 Jan 29 (6:30 PM)**: MACC detains DOE Director-General Wan Abdul Latiff Wan Jaffar and Deputy DG Dr. Norhazni Mat Sari at MACC headquarters in Putrajaya for investigation into e-waste corruption
- **2026 Jan 30**: Putrajaya Magistrate grants 7-day remand for three senior DOE officials (DG, deputy DG, and assistant officer) plus 3 days for a civilian; 16 bank accounts containing RM10.2 million frozen; seven companies in Selangor identified as linked to illegal e-waste trade
- **2026 Feb 4**: Malaysia imposes immediate nationwide ban on e-waste imports — reclassified under "absolute prohibition" category, ending all exemptions previously granted by DOE; MACC chief announces the ban
- **2026 Mar 4**: MACC announces investigation papers submitted to Deputy Public Prosecutor, with recommendation to prosecute DOE officials
- **2026 Mar 25**: MACC confirms investigation papers formally submitted to Attorney General's Chambers with prosecution recommendation
- **2026 Apr 1**: Customs officially gazettes absolute ban on e-waste imports under Customs (Prohibition of Imports) Order 2023
- **2026 Apr 1**: CSOs (Sahabat Alam Malaysia, C4) demand transparency from MACC and AGC on timing of charges, noting officials have not been suspended
- **2026 Apr 3**: DOE Director-General Datuk Wan Abdul Latiff Wan Jaffar, 58, charged at KL Sessions Court under Section 16(a)(B) of MACC Act 2009 for receiving RM100,000 bribe; pleads not guilty; bail set at RM40,000; passport surrendered
- **2026 Apr 3**: DOE Deputy DG Dr. Norhazni Mat Sari, 59, charged at KL Sessions Court with six counts under Section 165 of the Penal Code for receiving RM21,000 without consideration; pleads not guilty
- **2026 Apr 3**: Nationwide crackdown on banned e-waste begins — large-scale integrated operation led by Bukit Aman KDNKA; 800+ e-waste containers remain stranded at Port Klang
- **2026 Apr 5**: Norhazni scheduled to face additional corruption charge at Shah Alam court
- **2026 Apr 6**: Norhazni charged at Shah Alam Sessions Court with additional count — receiving RM9,110 for home renovations from Tang Siong Hee of E-Concern (Borneo) Sdn Bhd; pleads not guilty; total charges now seven

## RELEVANT LAW

- **Environmental Quality Act 1974 (Act 127)** — Malaysia's primary environmental legislation; Section 34A requires Environmental Impact Assessment for prescribed activities
- **Environmental Quality (Prescribed Activities) (Environmental Impact Assessment) Order 2015** — Lists activities requiring EIA; First Schedule (state DOE) and Second Schedule (DOE HQ)
- **Environmental Quality (Scheduled Wastes) Regulations 2005** — Classifies 77 types of scheduled wastes across 5 categories; e-waste classified as SW110
- **Environmental Quality (Amendment) Act 2024** — Enhanced penalties: mandatory imprisonment up to 5 years for river pollution; minimum fines RM50,000–RM100,000
- **MACC Act 2009, Section 16(a)(B)** — Corruptly receiving gratification; penalty: up to 20 years imprisonment and fine not less than 5x gratification or RM10,000; charge against DG
- **Penal Code, Section 165** — Public servant obtaining valuable thing without consideration; penalty: up to 2 years imprisonment, or fine, or both; charge against Deputy DG
- **Customs (Prohibition of Imports) Order 2023** — Amended April 1, 2026 to place e-waste under "absolute prohibition"
- **Basel Convention** — International treaty controlling transboundary hazardous waste movement; Malaysia is a signatory
