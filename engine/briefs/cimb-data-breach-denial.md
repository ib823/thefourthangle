# ISSUE — CIMB Denies 1.2M Record Breach — No Independent Audit

## PERIOD
March 2026 – April 2026

## CONTEXT (Timeline)

- **2010**: Malaysia enacts the Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA), one of the first comprehensive data protection laws in Southeast Asia; enforcement begins 2013
- **May 2022**: Payment gateway iPay88 confirms cybersecurity breach potentially compromising card data of users; Bank Negara Malaysia orders independent forensic investigation and instructs iPay88 to strengthen cybersecurity controls
- **Oct 2022**: JPN (National Registration Department) breach surfaces — alleged sale of 22.5 million Malaysians' personal data obtained through MyIdentity API, covering all adults born 1940–2004
- **Dec 2022**: Database of 13 million Malaysians allegedly obtained from Maybank, Astro, and Election Commission surfaces online; Communications Minister Fahmi orders probe; Maybank denies breach and claims systems are secure
- **Feb 2023**: Auditor-General report confirms MySejahtera data breach affected 3 million users — a "Super Admin" account downloaded personal data of 3 million vaccine recipients between Oct 28-31, 2021; Deputy Minister contradicts auditor's findings by claiming no breach occurred
- **Jul 2024**: Malaysia's Parliament passes Personal Data Protection (Amendment) Act 2024, introducing mandatory breach notification, DPO requirements, biometric data classification, and enhanced penalties up to RM1 million
- **Oct 2024**: PDPA Amendment Act 2024 enacted into law, with phased implementation
- **Jan 2025**: Phase 1 of PDPA amendments takes effect (administrative changes)
- **Feb 2025**: Personal Data Protection Commissioner releases first public list of compound cases — real enforcement actions against non-compliant data users
- **Apr 2025**: Phase 2 of PDPA amendments takes effect — "biometric data" classified as sensitive personal data; new cross-border data transfer rules
- **Apr 2025**: CIMB Philippines customers report unauthorized fund transfers of P500,000; bank attributes incident to phishing/social engineering and reimburses affected accounts
- **Jun 2025**: Phase 3 of PDPA amendments takes effect — mandatory DPO appointment, mandatory data breach notification within 72 hours, data portability rights
- **Aug 2025**: Hacker "Bigbrother" claims massive data theft from 11+ Malaysian ministries and government agencies on Dark Forums, priced at US$20,000
- **Nov 28, 2025**: Bank Negara Malaysia updates Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) policy with stricter incident reporting, 24/7 SOC requirements, and enhanced cyber resilience framework
- **Mar 2, 2026**: User "datasource" on Breachforums.as lists approximately 1,200,000 CIMB customer records for sale, describing them as "structured customer information extracted from internal systems"; sample field names posted include names, bank account numbers, card information, mobile numbers, gender, and dates of birth
- **Mar 3, 2026**: Cybersecurity monitoring services flag the listing; dark web intelligence firm Darknetsearch publishes analysis of the alleged leak
- **Mar 4, 2026**: CIMB Group Holdings Bhd publicly denies the breach on X (formerly Twitter), stating "customer data remains fully safeguarded" and "security teams have verified that all systems are secure"
- **Mar 4, 2026**: Multiple Malaysian outlets (The Star, The Edge, Bernama, FMT, Business Today, Lowyat) report on both the claim and the denial
- **Mar 4, 2026 onward**: A local cybersecurity consultant casts doubt on the legitimacy of the alleged leak, noting the data "appears to have been scraped from other, possibly unrelated, leaks rather than originating from a fresh breach"
- **As of Apr 6, 2026**: No independent forensic audit report published by CIMB; no third-party cybersecurity firm has validated or debunked the dataset; no statement issued by Bank Negara Malaysia, MCMC, NACSA, or the Personal Data Protection Commissioner regarding the CIMB claim; no law enforcement statement on record; no class action or formal legal challenge by customers

## ACTORS

- **CIMB Group Holdings Bhd** — Malaysia's second-largest banking group by assets; over 30 million customers across ASEAN; over 8 million customers in Malaysia alone; 217 branches nationwide; denied breach with a social media post but has not published technical indicators of compromise, sample record verification, or engaged a named third-party forensic auditor
- **"datasource" (Breachforums user)** — Pseudonymous threat actor who posted the listing on Mar 2, 2026; claimed 1.2 million records with structured fields; credibility unverified
- **Breachforums.as** — Underground cybercrime marketplace that emerged March 2022; hosted over 888 datasets containing more than 14 billion records per US DOJ filings; repeatedly disrupted by law enforcement; platform has known history of both genuine and fabricated data listings
- **Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM)** — Central bank and banking sector regulator; administers RMiT policy requiring banks to maintain 24/7 SOC, incident reporting, and post-incident reviews; has not issued any public advisory or statement regarding the CIMB claim
- **Personal Data Protection Commissioner (PDPC)** — Under Ministry of Communications and Digital; led by Professor Dr. Mohd Nazri bin Kama; administers the PDPA including the new mandatory 72-hour breach notification requirement effective June 2025
- **NACSA (National Cyber Security Agency)** — Administers Cyber Security Act 2024 (Act 854); oversees NCII entities including banking sector; requires 6-hour incident reports and 14-day follow-up reports; no public statement on the CIMB claim
- **MCMC (Malaysian Communications and Multimedia Commission)** — Communications regulator; no public statement on the CIMB claim
- **CyberSecurity Malaysia** — National cybersecurity technical agency; published Annual Report 2025 noting 42% year-over-year increase in ransomware attacks; average cost of data breach in Malaysia at RM3.2 million; operates MyCERT for incident response
- **Unnamed local cybersecurity consultant** — Quoted in media suggesting the data may have been scraped from previously circulated breaches rather than representing a fresh CIMB-specific compromise
- **CIMB customers (1.2M potentially affected)** — No organized legal response or class action to date; no public indication of whether individuals have been notified or offered credit monitoring

## RELEVANT LAW

- **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA)** — Malaysia's primary data protection legislation; establishes 7 principles (General, Notice and Choice, Disclosure, Security, Retention, Data Integrity, Access); penalty for breaching principles: fine up to RM300,000 and/or 2 years imprisonment (pre-amendment); prosecution requires written consent of Public Prosecutor
- **Personal Data Protection (Amendment) Act 2024** — Passed Jul 2024, enacted Oct 2024; phased implementation:
  - Phase 2 (Apr 2025): "biometric data" added as sensitive personal data; new cross-border transfer framework replacing whitelist approach
  - Phase 3 (Jun 2025): Mandatory breach notification to Commissioner within 72 hours (Section 12B); notification to affected data subjects within 7 days if significant harm likely; mandatory DPO appointment; data portability rights; enhanced penalties up to RM1,000,000 fine and/or 3 years imprisonment
  - Failure to notify breach: fine up to RM250,000 and/or 2 years imprisonment
  - Knowing/reckless collection or disclosure without consent: fine up to RM500,000 and/or 3 years imprisonment
  - Breach of significant scale (over 1,000 data subjects): mandatory Commissioner notification even without significant harm
- **Data Breach Notification Guidelines (Feb 2025)** — Issued by PDPC; defines "significant harm" including physical injury, financial loss, and exposure of banking details/login credentials
- **Cyber Security Act 2024 (Act 854)** — Effective Aug 26, 2024; banking and finance designated as one of 11 NCII sectors; NCII entities must report incidents to NACSA within 6 hours; follow-up report within 14 days; failure to report: fine up to RM500,000, imprisonment up to 10 years, or both
- **Bank Negara Malaysia RMiT Policy (updated Nov 2025)** — Risk Management in Technology framework for all financial institutions; mandates CISO designation, cyber resilience framework (CRF), technology risk management framework (TRMF), 24/7 SOC capability, prompt incident reporting to BNM, post-incident reviews, and long-term mitigations
- **BNM Guidelines on Management of Customer Information and Permitted Disclosure** — Requires financial service providers to promptly report data breaches that may undermine public confidence or harm reputation
- **Financial Services Act 2013** — Governs licensing and prudential requirements for financial institutions; BNM's enforcement authority over banks

## 12-DIMENSION RISK ASSESSMENT

1. **Sentiment** — HIGH: Financial data is deeply personal; "your bank says trust us but won't prove it" narrative resonates strongly; pattern of Malaysian institutions denying breaches only for confirmations to emerge later (cf. MySejahtera)
2. **Political** — MEDIUM: Government has invested political capital in PDPA amendments and digital economy narrative; a high-profile bank breach that goes uninvestigated could embarrass the administration's cybersecurity credentials
3. **Ethnic** — LOW: Not a primarily ethnic issue; CIMB serves all communities
4. **Religious** — LOW: Not a religious issue
5. **Narrative** — CRITICAL: The gap between Malaysia's impressive legislative framework (PDPA amendments, CSA 2024, RMiT) and the enforcement reality where a major bank can deny a breach with a tweet and face no mandatory independent verification
6. **Completeness** — MEDIUM: Thin factual record — only three confirmed elements (forum listing, data field descriptions, CIMB denial); no independent verification in either direction; this itself is a finding
7. **Temporal** — HIGH: The 72-hour mandatory notification requirement has been in effect since June 2025; the March 2026 incident is a direct test case
8. **Confidence** — MEDIUM: Breach claim unverified; Breachforums has documented fabricated listings; however CIMB's denial equally unverified; the story is the verification gap
9. **Sources** — HIGH: Well-documented by The Star, The Edge, Bernama, FMT, Lowyat, Business Today, specialist outlets
10. **Geographic** — MEDIUM: 8 million+ Malaysian customers potentially in scope
11. **Economic** — HIGH: CIMB is Malaysia's second-largest bank; unresolved allegations create reputational risk
12. **Gender** — LOW: Not a significantly gendered issue
