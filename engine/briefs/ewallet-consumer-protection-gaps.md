# E-Wallet Consumer Protection Gaps in Malaysia

## ISSUE
Despite 28 million Malaysians using e-wallets and RM48+ billion flowing through these platforms annually, e-wallet users enjoy dramatically fewer consumer protections than traditional bank account or credit card holders. Funds can be frozen without warning, there is no deposit insurance (PIDM), dispute resolution is opaque, and the "pain of paying" disappears — driving overspending among young adults.

## PERIOD
2023–2026 (focus on post-BNM revised e-money policy, January 2025 onwards)

## CONTEXT (Timeline)

- **2020–2021**: Government launches eTunai Rakyat (RM30 per person) and subsequent eMADANI programs, disbursing over RM2 billion through six national e-wallet subsidy schemes, onboarding millions of first-time digital payment users.
- **November 2023**: PayNet introduces DuitNow QR transaction fees (MDR) for merchants; BNM subsequently extends MDR waivers for micro/small businesses.
- **January 2025**: BNM's revised e-money policy takes effect, replacing 2022 guidelines — stricter governance, cybersecurity measures, mandatory 14-day refund policy for disputed transactions.
- **April–June 2025**: PDPA amendments roll out in three phases — mandatory breach notification within 72 hours, biometric data classified as sensitive, data portability rights introduced.
- **December 2025**: Multiple TNG eWallet users report mass account suspensions and frozen funds without warning; some users claim RM20,000+ frozen with no clear recourse.
- **January 2026**: Home Ministry confirms RM2.77 billion lost to online fraud in 2025 — highest in three years. Total 2023–2025 fraud losses: RM5.62 billion.
- **March 2026**: Commentary piece in Malay Mail argues Malaysia's scam economy is "a system design problem, not a public awareness problem."

## ACTORS

### Regulators
- **Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM)**: Central bank; issued revised e-money policy (Jan 2025); regulates e-money issuers
- **Personal Data Protection Department (PDPD)**: Enforces PDPA; rolled out Phase 2-3 amendments (Apr-Jun 2025)
- **Perbadanan Insurans Deposit Malaysia (PIDM)**: Deposit insurer — explicitly does NOT cover e-wallet balances
- **PayNet (Payments Network Malaysia)**: Operates DuitNow QR infrastructure; manages MDR fee structure
- **Securities Commission Malaysia**: Oversees fintech lending and investment platforms (overlapping jurisdiction)

### E-Wallet Operators
- **Touch 'n Go eWallet**: Market leader (~40% share); first to implement all 5 BNM safety measures; introduced Money-Back Guarantee; involved in mass suspension controversy (Dec 2025)
- **GrabPay Malaysia**: Second largest; integrated with ride-hailing ecosystem
- **Boost**: Third player; Axiata-backed
- **ShopeePay, MAE (Maybank), BigPay (AirAsia)**: Smaller but significant players
- **56 licensed providers total** (BNM expects consolidation to 8-12 by 2028)

### Affected Groups
- **28 million e-wallet users** nationally
- **B40 households**: Bottom 40% income group; many first onboarded through government subsidy programs
- **Elderly Malaysians**: Research shows significant adoption barriers (digital literacy, trust)
- **Rural communities**: 63.5% still relying on cash vs 36.2% in urban areas
- **Small merchants/hawkers**: MDR fees of 2%+ erode thin margins; many revert to cash after subsidy lapses
- **Young adults (Gen-Y/Gen-Z)**: Research shows e-wallets trigger impulse buying and overspending due to reduced "pain of paying"

## RELEVANT LAW

1. **Financial Services Act 2013 (FSA)** — BNM's authority to regulate e-money issuers
2. **BNM E-Money Policy Document (revised Jan 2025)** — Governance, cybersecurity, mandatory 14-day refund for disputed transactions, FOS membership requirement
3. **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA), as amended 2025** — Mandatory 72-hour breach notification; biometric data as sensitive; data portability rights; cross-border transfer rules
4. **PIDM Act 2011** — Deposit insurance explicitly excludes non-bank e-wallet balances
5. **Consumer Protection Act 1999** — General consumer rights, but no e-wallet-specific provisions
6. **Communications and Multimedia Act 1998 (CMA), Section 233** — Prohibits offensive/threatening online content
7. **Financial Ombudsman Scheme (FOS)** — Dispute resolution avenue; e-money issuers must register as members
