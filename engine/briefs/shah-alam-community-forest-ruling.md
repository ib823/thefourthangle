# ISSUE 1971 — A 22-Year Backdated Gazette Just Killed Shah Alam's Last Forest Corridor

## PERIOD
May 1909–April 2026

## CONTEXT (Timeline)
- May 1909: Bukit Cherakah Forest Reserve constituted — 23,826 acres (~9,642 hectares)
- 1991–2006: Selangor Forestry Department conducts degazetting process for 406.22 hectares; put on hold in 2006
- Nov 20, 2000: Selangor EXCO (BN government) decides to degazette area including SACF. No gazette notification published
- 2008: Selangor falls to opposition (Pakatan Rakyat/PH) — remains under PH since
- 2011: Selangor amends NFA adoption enactment — new Section 11 requires mandatory public inquiry before any forest excision. Only state with this requirement
- Mar 2016: SACF Society formally established to preserve the forest
- 2019: SACF Society receives US$47,000 UNDP GEF Small Grants Programme for biodiversity research
- Jan 2020: UNDP-funded biodiversity survey begins — documents 316 species, 40 on IUCN Red List
- May 2020: Planning permission approved for road linking Jalan Pulau Angsa U10/39 to Persiaran Setia Duta (Setia Ecopark)
- May 5, 2022: Selangor publishes Gazette Notification No. 1645 — degazettes 406.22 hectares, backdated to the Nov 2000 EXCO decision, bypassing the 2011 public inquiry requirement
- Jul 18, 2022: 53 CSOs issue joint statement demanding immediate revocation
- Aug 4, 2022: SACF Society and PEKA file judicial review at Shah Alam High Court
- Nov 17, 2022: High Court dismisses judicial review
- Jan 24, 2024: Court of Appeal dismisses appeal
- Sep 9, 2024: Federal Court grants leave to appeal unanimously — Justice Abdul Rahman Sebli: "questions raise questions of importance on which further argument and a decision of this court will be to the public advantage"
- Jul 25, 2025: Justice Abdul Rahman Sebli retires
- Dec 21, 2025: ~100 Arundina Setia Eco Park residents stage peaceful protest
- Feb 5, 2026: 680-signature petition presented to MB Amirudin Shari
- Mar 3, 2026: Road earthworks reported 70% complete (1km through SACF)
- Apr 1, 2026: Federal Court delivers 2-1 split decision dismissing the appeal on standing grounds

## ACTORS
- **Federal Court** — Split 2-1: majority (Abu Bakar Jais CJ, Rhodzariah Bujang FCJ) vs dissent (Lee Swee Seng FCJ)
- **Justice Abdul Rahman Sebli** — Granted leave to appeal Sept 2024; retired July 2025 before substantive hearing
- **SACF Society** — Lead appellant; est. March 2016; received UNDP biodiversity funding
- **PEKA (Pertubuhan Pelindung Khazanah Alam Malaysia)** — Co-appellant
- **53 CSOs** led by B.E.A.C.C.H. cluster — Joint statement July 2022 (includes WWF-Malaysia, SAM, CAP, KAMY, RimbaWatch, Wild Bird Club, COAC, Friends of Bukit Kiara/Gasing)
- **Malaysian Bar** — Press release demanding due process in forest excision
- **Selangor State Government (PH/PKR)** — Respondent; published backdated gazette
- **Datuk Seri Amirudin Shari** — Selangor MB (PKR); received petition; also reversed KLNFR degazettement in 2021
- **MBSA (Shah Alam City Council)** — Approved planning permission; received 718 objections against road, 195 against cemetery
- **PKNS (Selangor State Development Corporation)** — Owns ~300 acres (70%) of SACF land
- **SP Setia** — Developer of Setia Ecopark; road connects to their development and DASH highway
- **Temuan Orang Asli** of Kampung Ayer Kuning — Indigenous community with traditional territory in forest; not separately represented in litigation
- **Setia Arundina residents** — Led protests; Jeffrey Heng presented petition

## RELEVANT LAW
- **National Forestry Act 1984** — Federal framework; land/forestry is state matter (9th Schedule, State List)
- **Selangor NFA (Adoption) Enactment 1985, Section 11** — Amended 2011 to require mandatory public inquiry before degazettement. Selangor only state with this requirement
- **Selangor NFA Section 12** — Requires replacement forest land of equal or greater size when forest excised. No replacement gazetted for SACF
- **Selangor Public Inquiry (Selangor) Rules 2014** — Procedural rules for Section 11 inquiries
- **Town and Country Planning Act 1976** — MBSA planning approval authority
- **Wildlife Conservation Act 2010** — Protects species found in SACF (tapir, gibbon, leopard cat: "totally protected")
- **Federal Constitution, 9th Schedule** — Land and forestry are state matters
- **Environmental Quality Act 1974** — EIA requirements

## 12-DIMENSION RISK ASSESSMENT
1. **Sentiment** — HIGH: Strong community anger; environmental groups mobilized; but mainstream fatigue after years of litigation
2. **Political** — HIGH: PH government defending BN-era decision exposes reformist credibility gap; same MB reversed KLNFR but not SACF
3. **Ethnic** — MEDIUM: Temuan Orang Asli rights invisible in litigation; no ethnic framing in mainstream coverage
4. **Religious** — LOW: Not a religious issue; cemetery component (Muslim burial ground on degazetted land) has minor sensitivity
5. **Narrative** — CRITICAL: "NGOs lose court case" framing buries the real story — the backdating loophole, the standing trap, and the precedent for all Malaysian forests
6. **Completeness** — HIGH: The substantive legal question (is backdating lawful?) was never adjudicated. Road continued during litigation. Section 12 replacement land never provided
7. **Temporal** — CRITICAL: Decision just delivered April 1, 2026; road 70% complete; forest corridor being severed now
8. **Confidence** — HIGH: Court documents, official gazettes, UNDP-funded biodiversity data, multiple independent media sources
9. **Sources** — HIGH: Malay Mail, FMT, The Star, EdgeProp, Eco-Business, NST, Malaysiakini, WWF-Malaysia, Malaysian Bar, UNDP
10. **Geographic** — MEDIUM: Selangor-specific but precedent affects all states nationally
11. **Economic** — HIGH: PKNS development interests vs community amenity; SP Setia property value uplift from road; no public cost-benefit analysis
12. **Gender** — LOW: Not primarily gendered; women participate in community activism but not a distinct angle
