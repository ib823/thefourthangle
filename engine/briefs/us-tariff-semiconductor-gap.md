# ISSUE 1965 — Supreme Court Killed the Tariff Deal — Now Malaysia Faces Something Worse

## PERIOD
April 2025–April 2026

## CONTEXT (Timeline)
- Apr 2, 2025: Trump announces 24% reciprocal tariff on Malaysia under International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA)
- Apr 9, 2025: 90-day pause on higher tariffs for all countries except China
- Oct 26, 2025: Accelerated Reciprocal Trade (ART) deal signed in Kuala Lumpur — tariff reduced from 24% to ~19% on Malaysian exports
- Feb 20, 2026: US Supreme Court strikes down ALL IEEPA-based tariffs 6-3 in *Learning Resources v. Trump* — rules IEEPA does not authorize trade tariffs
- Feb 24, 2026: All IEEPA tariffs terminated at midnight per court order
- Post-SCOTUS: Trump administration imposes blanket 10% tariff on all imports via Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974 (150-day emergency authority)
- Mar 11, 2026: USTR launches Section 301 investigation into 16 economies including Malaysia for "excess manufacturing capacity"
- Mar 13, 2026: Second Section 301 probe targeting 60 economies over forced labor in supply chains
- Mar 15, 2026: Malaysia's Trade Minister Johari Abdul Ghani declares the ART trade deal "null and void" — Malaysia walks out
- Mar 16, 2026: Fortune reports Asia "scrambles to respond" to Section 301 probes
- Mar 2026: Government unveils RM1.5B (US$356M) SME relief package
- Ongoing: 10% blanket tariff in effect + dual Section 301 probes building legal infrastructure for new targeted tariffs that could exceed original 24%

## RELEVANT LAW
- **International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA)** — Originally used by Trump to impose 24% tariff; struck down by SCOTUS as exceeding statutory authority
- **Learning Resources v. Trump (2026)** — US Supreme Court, 6-3, ruled IEEPA does not authorize trade tariffs; voided all IEEPA-based tariff orders
- **Section 122, Trade Act of 1974** — 150-day emergency tariff authority; Trump's fallback for blanket 10% tariff; limited to 15% max rate and 150-day duration without Congressional renewal
- **Section 301, Trade Act of 1974** — Authorizes tariffs against "unfair trade practices"; no SCOTUS vulnerability because explicitly designed for trade; no cap on tariff rate or duration; requires investigation period before imposition
- **Accelerated Reciprocal Trade (ART) deal** — Bilateral agreement signed Oct 26, 2025; reduced tariff from 24% to ~19%; declared "null and void" by Malaysia on Mar 15, 2026 after IEEPA tariffs struck down
- **CPTPP** — Malaysia is member; US is not; provides no bilateral tariff protection
- **Investment Incentives Act 1986** — Malaysia's FDI attraction framework
