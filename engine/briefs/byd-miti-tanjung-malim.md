ISSUE: BYD Halts Tanjung Malim EV Plant Over MITI's Protectionist Conditions
PERIOD: August 2025 (announcement) – March 30, 2026 (stalled)

CONTEXT:
- Aug 22, 2025: BYD confirmed CKD assembly plant at KLK TechPark, Tanjung Malim, Perak. 150 acres. Production target H2 2026. (Malay Mail, Paultan, KLK Land)
- KLK TechPark is a 1,500-acre integrated industrial hub with RM3.5 billion gross development value over 10 years. BYD is anchor investor. (The Edge Malaysia, TechNode Global)
- Mar 30, 2026: BYD "rethinking" Malaysia plans — cannot agree to MITI conditions. Plant progress has "ground to a halt." (Paultan, FMT, Malay Mail, Rakyat Post, Piston)
- MITI conditions: (1) 80% of vehicles produced must be exported. (2) Remaining 20% for domestic market must be priced above RM200,000 per unit. (Paultan, Malay Mail, AutoBuzz)
- MITI Minister Datuk Seri Johari Abdul Ghani confirmed: "These were the terms they couldn't agree on." Cited need to protect local automotive industry employing ~700,000 people. (Malay Mail, FMT)
- Johari stated Proton sells ~150,000 units/year, Perodua ~350,000 units/year, both with 50% local content. (Malay Mail)
- Dec 31, 2025: MITI also set RM250,000 minimum price floor for CBU EVs from new brands entering Malaysia, up from RM100,000. (Paultan, AutoBuzz)
- CKD EVs from Zeekr, Xpeng, and MG to enjoy full tax exemption until Dec 31, 2027. Zeekr assembling 7X at AHTV Tanjung Malim — Malaysia is first country outside China to assemble Zeekr. (Paultan, Malay Mail, Motoqar)
- Xpeng G6/X9 to be assembled at EPMB Pegoh, Melaka. (Paultan)
- Automotive industry contributes 4% of GDP (~RM40 billion) and employs 700,000+ in nationwide ecosystem. (MIDA, Wikipedia)
- Perodua has ~130 local vendors, directly employs 11,500, total ecosystem ~70,000. (Perodua, Wikipedia)
- BYD is world's largest EV maker by volume. In 2025, BYD sold over 4.2 million vehicles globally. (Reuters)
- BYD Atto 3 and Dolphin currently sold in Malaysia as CBU imports priced RM130K-170K — well below RM200K threshold. (Paultan)
- If BYD CKD proceeds with RM200K floor: eliminates BYD's competitive advantage (affordable EVs). Only premium models (Seal, Han) viable.
- Consumer impact: Malaysians denied access to affordable locally-assembled EVs. B40/M40 EV transition blocked.

ACTORS:
- BYD Auto (China) — world's largest EV maker, anchor investor at KLK TechPark
- Datuk Seri Johari Abdul Ghani — MITI Minister, set protectionist conditions
- KLK Land — developer of KLK TechPark (1,500 acres, RM3.5B GDV)
- Proton — national carmaker, 150K units/year, 50% local content, 49.9% owned by Geely (China)
- Perodua — national carmaker, 350K units/year, 50% local content, 25.2% owned by Daihatsu (Japan)
- Zeekr — Chinese EV brand, first CKD assembly outside China at AHTV Tanjung Malim
- Xpeng — Chinese EV brand, CKD at EPMB Pegoh Melaka
- Perak state government — invested in BYD plant as economic transformation anchor
- Malaysian consumers — particularly B40/M40 seeking affordable EV transition

RELEVANT LAW:
- National Automotive Policy (NAP) 2020 — MITI's framework for industry development
- Industrial Coordination Act 1975 — manufacturing licence conditions
- Customs (Prohibition of Imports) Order 2023 — CBU import conditions for EVs
- EV tax exemptions: 100% sales tax + excise duty exemption for CKD EVs until Dec 31, 2027
