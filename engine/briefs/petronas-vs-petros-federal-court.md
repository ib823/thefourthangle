# ISSUE 1975 — Petronas vs Petros: Both Sides Go to Federal Court Simultaneously — The RM2 Trillion Constitutional Showdown

## PERIOD
January 2024 – April 2026

## CONTEXT (Timeline)

- **1954/1958**: Sarawak (Alteration of Boundaries) Order in Council 1954 and Sarawak (Definition of Boundaries) Order in Council 1958 establish Sarawak's continental shelf boundaries under British colonial rule.
- **16 September 1963**: Malaysia formed under the Malaysia Agreement 1963 (MA63). Sarawak's pre-existing Oil Mining Ordinance 1958 preserved under Section 73 of the Malaysia Act 1963. Sovereign rights over petroleum resources in the seabed within Sarawak boundaries vested in Sarawak — not the Federation — by the British Government on Malaysia Day, per Sarawak's legal argument.
- **13 May 1969**: Racial riots lead to Emergency Proclamation. Federal government unilaterally limits Borneo states' territorial waters to 3 nautical miles. Continental Shelf Act 1966 and Petroleum Mining Act 1966 — originally applicable only to Peninsular Malaysia — extended to Sarawak through emergency ordinance.
- **1974**: Petroleum Development Act (PDA) 1974 enacted during Emergency, vesting exclusive ownership and control of all petroleum resources in PETRONAS. Enacted without endorsement from Sarawak's Dewan Undangan Negeri (DUN). PDA simultaneously introduced the 5% royalty to oil-producing states.
- **1974–2021**: Sabah's 40% revenue entitlement under Article 112C frozen. Special grant remains at RM26.7 million annually from the 1969 review — no further mandatory review ever conducted.
- **December 2011**: Both Houses of Parliament annul the 1969 Emergency Proclamation. Sarawak argues Continental Shelf Act 1966 and Petroleum Mining Act 1966 lapsed in application to Sarawak by June 2012 under Article 150(7) of the Federal Constitution.
- **24 July 2017**: Petroleum Sarawak Berhad (PETROS) incorporated as a state-owned oil and gas company, wholly owned by the Sarawak government. Launched 6 March 2018.
- **2022**: PETROS becomes the third-largest equity oil and gas producer in Malaysia.
- **August 2024**: Shell MDS receives invoices from both Petronas and Petros for the same gas supply, triggering the payment dispute that exposed the jurisdictional crisis.
- **15 October 2024**: Petros files originating summons against Petronas over RM7.95 million Maybank bank guarantee, claiming the Sarawak Gas Sales Agreement (SGSA) is "illegal and void" because Petronas failed to obtain licensing under Sarawak's Distribution of Gas Ordinance (DGO) 2016.
- **January 2025**: Shell MDS obtains interim injunction to suspend payments to both parties.
- **1 March 2025**: Petros becomes effective sole gas aggregator under Section 7A of the DGO 2016.
- **29 April 2025**: ConocoPhillips withdraws from the US$3.1 billion (RM13.7 billion) Salam-Patawali deepwater project — a 50-50 joint venture with Petronas discovered in 2018 — amid regulatory uncertainty from the Petronas-Sarawak dispute.
- **9 May 2025**: PM Anwar Ibrahim and Premier Abang Johari Openg meet and agree that Petros will be the domestic gas aggregator in Sarawak while Petronas handles LNG exports. Framework collapses within months.
- **22 September 2025**: Court of Appeal overturns High Court, rules Petronas did not breach gas supply agreement with Shell MDS. Orders Shell MDS to pay all outstanding sums from August 2024 to October 2025.
- **17 October 2025**: Kota Kinabalu High Court landmark ruling — Justice Celestina Stuel Galid rules Sabah constitutionally entitled to 40% of net federal revenue collected from the state under Articles 112C and 112D. Issues mandamus order: fresh review within 90 days, agreement including back payments for "lost years" (1974–2021) within 180 days (deadline: 15 April 2026).
- **12 January 2026**: PETRONAS files motion at Federal Court in Putrajaya under Article 4(4) of the Federal Constitution, seeking clarity on the regulatory framework governing its operations in Sarawak. Challenges the competency of several Sarawak state laws.
- **13 January 2026**: Premier Abang Johari responds: "Let the courts decide" — urges restraint, says apex court should adjudicate, Sarawak will respect the rule of law.
- **21 January 2026**: Five Petronas subsidiaries (Malaysia LNG Sdn Bhd, Malaysia LNG Dua Sdn Bhd, Malaysia LNG Tiga Sdn Bhd, Petronas Carigali Sdn Bhd, Petronas LNG 9 Sdn Bhd) file judicial review over RM120 million penalties imposed by Sarawak's Ministry of Utility and Telecommunication for failing to apply for eight licences under the DGO 2016.
- **5 February 2026**: Court of Appeal clarifies payment order wording in Shell MDS gas dispute.
- **6 February 2026**: Kuching High Court grants five Petronas subsidiaries leave to challenge RM120 million penalties.
- **23 February 2026**: Sarawak government files its own Federal Court petition under Articles 4(3) and 128(1) of the Federal Constitution, challenging the constitutional validity and applicability to Sarawak of three federal laws: the Petroleum Development Act 1974, the Continental Shelf Act 1966, and the Petroleum Mining Act 1966. Raises three constitutional grounds.
- **25 February 2026**: Kuching High Court dismisses Petros' challenge of Petronas' call on RM7.95 million bank guarantee, with RM50,000 costs to Petronas.
- **27 February 2026**: PETRONAS announces FY2025 results: net profit down 18% to RM45.4 billion; revenue down 17% to RM266.1 billion. Dividend to government set at RM20 billion for 2026 — lowest since 2017, down 38% from RM32 billion in 2025.
- **3 March 2026**: Federal Attorney General applies for stay of Sabah 40% High Court order.
- **8 March 2026**: Brent crude surpasses US$100/barrel for first time in four years amid Strait of Hormuz crisis, eventually peaking at ~US$126/barrel. Global LNG supply cut by ~20%.
- **16 March 2026**: Federal Court grants Petronas leave to challenge Sarawak state laws. Chief Judge of Malaya Datuk Seri Hashim Hamzah rules Petronas met the threshold for "a strong arguable case." Petronas given 21 days to file motion for petition. Federal Government does not oppose Petronas' leave application.
- **18 March 2026**: Sarawak Deputy Minister says state ready to file own petition to protect Petros, seeks joint hearing with Petronas petition.
- **6 April 2026**: Court of Appeal grants Putrajaya's stay on Sabah 40% revenue ruling — the April 15 deadline for agreement is suspended pending appeal.

## RELEVANT LAW

- **Malaysia Agreement 1963 (MA63)** — Treaty establishing Malaysia. Sarawak argues sovereign petroleum rights were vested in the state, not the federation, on Malaysia Day.
- **Federal Constitution Article 4(3) and 4(4)** — Jurisdiction for constitutional validity challenges.
- **Federal Constitution Article 128(1)** — Federal Court's original jurisdiction over constitutional questions.
- **Federal Constitution Article 95D** — No Act of Parliament on State List matters applies to Sarawak unless adopted by State Legislative Assembly.
- **Federal Constitution Article 150(7)** — Emergency laws lapse six months after Emergency annulment.
- **Federal Constitution Articles 112C and 112D** — Sabah's 40% revenue entitlement and mandatory periodic review.
- **Federal Constitution Ninth Schedule** — Item 2(c) vests legislative authority in Sarawak's DUN to regulate petroleum rights through mining leases and licences.
- **Petroleum Development Act 1974 (PDA 1974)** — Vests exclusive ownership of all petroleum resources in PETRONAS. Central target of Sarawak's challenge.
- **Continental Shelf Act 1966** — Originally applicable to Peninsular Malaysia only. Extended to Sarawak via Emergency. Sarawak argues it lapsed after 2011 Emergency annulment.
- **Petroleum Mining Act 1966** — Same status as Continental Shelf Act.
- **Oil Mining Ordinance 1958 (Sarawak)** — Pre-Malaysia Sarawak law governing petroleum. Preserved under Section 73 of Malaysia Act 1963.
- **Distribution of Gas Ordinance (DGO) 2016 (amended 2023)** — Sarawak state law establishing Petros as sole gas aggregator.
- **Environment (Reduction of Greenhouse Gas Emission) Ordinance 2023** — Sarawak's carbon legislation also at stake.
- **Sarawak (Alteration of Boundaries) Order in Council 1954 / (Definition of Boundaries) Order in Council 1958** — British-era instruments defining Sarawak continental shelf boundaries.

## 12-DIMENSION RISK ASSESSMENT

1. **Sentiment** — CRITICAL: Deep emotional resonance in Borneo. 50 years of resource extraction with 5% royalty. Risk of anti-federal sentiment escalating.
2. **Political** — CRITICAL: GPS holds 23 seats essential to Anwar's coalition. Federal Court ruling either way has massive political fallout.
3. **Ethnic** — MEDIUM: Borneo grievances cross ethnic lines within Sarawak. "Borneo vs Malaya" framing risks regional tensions. Dayak identity linked to resource sovereignty.
4. **Religious** — LOW: Not primarily a religious issue.
5. **Narrative** — CRITICAL: Two competing narratives. Sarawak: "Colonial-era injustice perpetuated since 1974." Petronas/Federal: "National energy security requires unified framework."
6. **Completeness** — HIGH: Coverage underreports Emergency lapse argument, Sabah's parallel claim, and ConocoPhillips withdrawal signaling investor flight.
7. **Temporal** — CRITICAL: Multiple converging deadlines. Petronas petition, Sabah appeal, Hormuz crisis, GE16 looming.
8. **Confidence** — HIGH: Bloomberg, SCMP, The Edge, Malay Mail, Borneo Post, PETRONAS releases, court filings.
9. **Sources** — HIGH: 26 credible sources.
10. **Geographic** — HIGH: Sarawak-centric but national implications. Bintulu is a national strategic asset.
11. **Economic** — CRITICAL: RM2.25 trillion historical extraction, RM20B dividend at risk, RM13.7B investment fled. If both Borneo claims succeed, hundreds of billions in restructuring.
12. **Gender** — LOW: Not a gendered issue, though resource extraction impacts on indigenous women underreported.
