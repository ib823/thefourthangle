# ISSUE 1961 — MACC Chief Under Investigation: Who Watches Malaysia's Watchdog?

## PERIOD
February–April 2026

## CONTEXT (Timeline)
- 2022: First allegations surface about Azam Baki's shareholdings exceeding RM100K civil servant limit
- Feb 11, 2026: Bloomberg publishes exposé alleging MACC working with businessmen to seize control of companies
- Feb 13, 2026: Cabinet orders investigation into MACC Chief Azam Baki
- Feb 20, 2026: Civil society groups (Bersih, Bar Council, etc.) demand Royal Commission of Inquiry
- Feb 21, 2026: Bloomberg reports MACC chief's defamation suit — $25M against Bloomberg LP
- Feb 24, 2026: Senior minister breaks ranks, publicly calls for RCI
- Feb 27, 2026: PM Anwar says cabinet discussed RCI, defers to AG-led committee findings
- Mar 3, 2026: Anwar says findings from special probe panel expected "this week"
- Mar 11, 2026: The Star editorial: "About time for a royal inquiry"
- Apr 1, 2026: Fahmi confirms investigation "still ongoing," updates to be disclosed publicly

## ACTORS
- Tan Sri Azam Baki — MACC Chief Commissioner, subject of probe
- PM Anwar Ibrahim — Defender of MACC institution, deferred RCI decision
- Fahmi Fadzil — Communications Minister, confirmed ongoing investigation
- Attorney General's office — Leading the investigation committee
- Bloomberg LP — Published exposé, facing $25M defamation suit
- Bersih, Malaysian Bar Council, civil society — Demanding RCI
- Senior minister (unnamed in some reports) — Broke ranks calling for RCI
- Tun Tengku Maimun — Former Chief Justice, proposed to lead independent probe

## RELEVANT LAW
- MACC Act 2009 — Establishes MACC independence and commissioner's powers
- Public Officers (Conduct and Discipline) Regulations — RM100K shareholding limit for civil servants
- Commissions of Enquiry Act 1950 — Empowers Yang di-Pertuan Agong to establish RCI
- CMA Section 233 — Used in Bloomberg defamation context
- Federal Constitution Article 145 — AG's role and prosecutorial discretion

## KEY STATISTICS
- RM100,000 — Maximum shareholding allowed for public servants (Azam alleged to exceed this)
- $25 million — Defamation suit filed by Azam against Bloomberg
- $13 million — Cash/gold/luxury goods seized in related military corruption raids (separate but concurrent MACC credibility issue)
- 47 — Transparency International CPI ranking for Malaysia (declining)
- RM47 billion — Estimated annual cost of corruption in Malaysia (3% GDP)
