# ISSUE 1043 — When States Can Override Environmental Law, the Environment Has No Government

## PERIOD
1984–April 2026

## CONTEXT (Timeline)
- 1957: Federal Constitution 9th Schedule divides powers — land/forestry under State List; "environment" not defined in Constitution
- 1974: Environmental Quality Act enacted (federal) — EIA requirements, but DOE role is advisory only
- 1984: National Forestry Act — federal framework, but states must voluntarily adopt amendments
- 2003: Federal government bans logging in Ulu Muda — Kedah later overrides after state government change
- 2008: Kedah resumes Ulu Muda logging under PAS state government
- 2011: Selangor introduces mandatory public inquiry for degazettement — only state to do so
- 2013-2016: Pahang bauxite boom operates entirely without EIA; 15km coastline contaminated
- Dec 2014: Johor executes largest one-time excision — 15,011 hectares from Jemaluang/Tenggaroh
- Jan 2016: Johor, Kelantan, Kedah lose forest management certification
- Oct 2017: Tanjung Bungah landslide kills 11 after DOE objection ignored by Penang OSC
- Aug 2021: KLNFR degazetted despite 45,423 objections; reversed Sep 2021 after backlash
- May 2022: SACF backdated gazette published — 406 hectares from Bukit Cherakah
- Sep 2022: NFA Amendment Act 2022 passed — mandatory public inquiry, replacement forest
- Dec 2022: Batang Kali landslide kills 31 in EIA-violated area; Cameron Highlands mudslide same month
- Jul 2024: Environmental Quality (Amendment) Act 2024 — stricter EIA, but doesn't override state land power
- 2025: Only 3 jurisdictions adopt NFA 2022 (Perlis, Selangor, Federal Territories) — 8 states refuse
- 2024: RimbaWatch reports at-risk forests grew from 2.3M to 3.2M hectares in one year
- Apr 2026: SACF Federal Court dismisses appeal 2-1 on standing — backdating legality never adjudicated

## ACTORS
- **DOE (Department of Environment)** — Federal body; approves EIA reports but cannot veto state land decisions
- **State governments** (Selangor, Johor, Kedah, Kelantan, Pahang, Penang, Perak) — exercise land/forestry powers
- **MBSA, OSC committees** — Local planning authorities that can ignore DOE recommendations
- **SAM/FOE Malaysia (Sahabat Alam Malaysia)** — Leading environmental NGO; demanded NFA adoption
- **Malaysian Bar** — Called for constitutional reform to include "environment" in Federal List
- **Prof. Ainul Jaria Maidin (IIUM)** — Key academic: "planning authorities are not required to follow DOE recommendations"
- **WWF-Malaysia** — Joint media releases on SACF and forest governance
- **RimbaWatch** — Documented 3.2M hectares at risk (2024)
- **Macaranga** — Investigative journalism on excisions (Pulitzer Center partnership)

## KEY STATISTICS
- **725,613 ha** — Satellite-measured primary forest loss, Peninsular Malaysia 2001-2019
- **245,566 ha** — Official forest loss figure for same period (3x discrepancy)
- **3.2 million ha** — Forests at risk of deforestation nationally (RimbaWatch 2024, up from 2.3M in 2023)
- **17,630 ha** — Largest combined excision (Johor Jemaluang/Tenggaroh 2012-2014)
- **42 deaths** — Tanjung Bungah (11) + Batang Kali (31) in landslides linked to EIA bypass/violations
- **64%** — Non-compliance rate with EIA conditions in Penang inspections
- **3 of 11** — Peninsular states adopting NFA Amendment Act 2022 after 3+ years
- **RM46.7M** — Pahang bauxite royalties collected during period with zero EIA
- **45,423** — Objections against KLNFR (reversed) vs 718 against SACF (not reversed)
