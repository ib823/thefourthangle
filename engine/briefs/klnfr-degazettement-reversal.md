# ISSUE 1580 — 45,000 Objections Saved One Forest. 718 Could Not Save the Next. Same Government.

## PERIOD
1927–April 2026

## CONTEXT (Timeline)
- 1886: Temuan Orang Asli documented using the forest
- 1927: KLNFR gazetted — 7,246.96 hectares; gazette expressly admitted Orang Asli rights
- Over decades: Reserve shrinks from 7,247 ha to ~957.6 ha (87% lost)
- 1993-94: Busut Baru Orang Asli relocated for KLIA; promised 404.7 ha compensation — never delivered
- Feb 2020: Selangor Forestry Dept proposes degazetting ~931 ha (97% of remaining reserve)
- Mar 2020: 30-day objection period ends with 45,423 formal objections
- Nov 2020: Selangor State Assembly unanimously passes motion to protect gazetted forest reserves
- Dec 2018-Nov 2020: Developer entities created — Titian Jutaria (royal-linked), then Gabungan Indah (RM1 capital, Samling Group chain)
- May 2021: Selangor EXCO decides to degazette
- Aug 12, 2021: Degazettement gazetted — 536.7 hectares legally degazetted
- Aug 30, 2021: Decision announced publicly; massive backlash erupts
- Aug 31, 2021: Anwar summons MB Amirudin; PKR had rejected proposal the previous year
- Sep 8, 2021: MB reverses — halts development, announces regazettement
- Jun 3, 2023: Regazettement of 536.7 ha finalised; 42 ha remains degazetted for Orang Asli Busut Baru
- Feb 2023: ECRL approved via northern alignment, not through KLNFR
- Feb 2026: Peat fire at neighbouring Kuala Langat South reserve — 22.66 ha affected

## ACTORS
- **MB Amirudin Shari (PKR)** — Approved degazettement; reversed under party pressure
- **Anwar Ibrahim** — Summoned Amirudin; PKR had rejected proposal
- **Titian Jutaria Sdn Bhd** — Would receive 607 ha; directors included Tengku Amir Shah (now Sultan, then Crown Prince) and Syed Budriz; Sultan transferred 40% stake after scandal; TJSB dissolved
- **Gabungan Indah Sdn Bhd** — RM1 capital; single director former Johor UMNO Youth chief; owned via Vibrantscape → Perdana Parkcity → Samling Group
- **MBI Selangor** — Would receive 364 ha; state investment arm
- **PHSKLU coalition** — Pertahankan Hutan Simpan KLU; multi-generational NGO coalition
- **Temuan Orang Asli** — Four settlements (Bukit Kecil, Bukit Cheeding, Busut Baru, Pulau Kempas); ~1,000-2,000 affected
- **Malaysian Bar** — Called for full consideration of Orang Asli land rights under Aboriginal Peoples Act 1954
- **GEC (Global Environment Centre)** — Fire prevention, canal blocks, piezometers

## KEY STATISTICS
- **7,247 ha** → **957.6 ha** — 87% of original reserve lost before 2020 controversy
- **536.7 ha** — Degazetted Aug 2021; regazetted Jun 2023
- **45,423** — Formal objections (also reported as 43,502 by SAM)
- **~130,000** — Total online petition signatures
- **8,000 years** — Age of peat formation
- **1.5 million tonnes** — Carbon stored in KLNFR peat
- **5.5 million tonnes CO2** — Estimated release if cleared/drained
- **ESA Rank 1** — National Physical Plan designation (no development)
- **42 ha** — Remains degazetted for Busut Baru Orang Asli land entitlement
- **404.7 ha** — Originally promised to Busut Baru in 1994; only ~42 ha delivered (10%)
