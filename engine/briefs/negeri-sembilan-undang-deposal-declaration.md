# ISSUE 1976 — Negeri Sembilan Undang Deposal Declaration

## ISSUE
On 19 April 2026, a former Undang Luak Sungai Ujong, Datuk Mubarak Dohak, signed and read a declaration purporting to depose the 11th Yang di-Pertuan Besar (YDPB) of Negeri Sembilan, Tuanku Muhriz Tuanku Munawir, at the Balai Undang Luak Sungai Ujong. The next day, Negeri Sembilan Menteri Besar Datuk Seri Aminuddin Harun rejected the declaration as having "no authority or validity" on three grounds: (a) Mubarak no longer holds the office of Undang Luak; (b) the declaration is inconsistent with Articles 10 to 12 of the Undang-Undang Tubuh Kerajaan Negeri Sembilan (UUTKNS) 1959; (c) customary-law bodies representing the Telaga Undang itself called the act "menderhaka" (treasonous). The historical irony: Mubarak Dohak was the same Undang Luak Sungai Ujong who proclaimed Tuanku Muhriz as the 11th YDPB on 29 December 2008.

## PERIOD
29 December 2008 (Tuanku Muhriz proclaimed 11th YDPB) — 20 April 2026 (MB rejects deposal declaration). Immediate window: May 2025 – April 2026.

## CONTEXT (Timeline)

- **14 April 1967**: The 9th YDPB Tuanku Munawir dies. His only son, Tunku Muhriz (born 14 January 1948), is bypassed by the Council of Undangs, who elect Muhriz's uncle Tuanku Ja'afar as 10th YDPB. This outcome reflects the non-hereditary nature of the NS throne — consensus among the Undangs prevails over primogeniture. Tunku Muhriz retains his title as Tunku Besar and undertakes ceremonial/advisory roles. (Wikipedia: Muhriz of NS; Negeri Sembilan Government official portal)
- **27 December 2008**: Tuanku Ja'afar (10th YDPB) dies.
- **29 December 2008, 3:45pm**: At Istana Besar Seri Menanti, **Undang Luak Sungai Ujong Datuk Klana Petra Datuk Mubarak Dohak** proclaims Tuanku Muhriz as the 11th Yang di-Pertuan Besar of Negeri Sembilan, chosen from among the "Putera Yang Empat" (the Four Princes) per Adat Perpatih and Articles 7 and 11 UUTKNS 1959. (mStar, Malaysia Today, NS Government portal)
- **27 March 2016**: Precedent event — Undang Luak Johol, Datuk Mohammad Jan Abdul Ghani (the 14th), is dismissed from office under Clause 14(3) UUTKNS for 13 offences against the customs and traditions of Luak Johol. The Dewan Keadilan dan Undang (DKU) accepts the dismissal. This establishes that Undang Luak *can* be removed — but does not establish any symmetric power of a (former) Undang to remove a YDPB. (MB Aminuddin's own reference, Apr 20 2026, via BERNAMA / FMT / Malay Mail)
- **13 May 2025**: Datuk Mubarak Dohak is dismissed as Datuk Klana Petra Luak Sungai Ujong on 33 offences against the customs and traditions of the Telaga Undang, Waris Klana Hulu, Sungei Ujong, under Clause 14(3) UUTKNS. The specific nature of the 33 offences has not been publicly disclosed by either the Telaga Undang or the state government. (Malay Mail, Apr 20 2026, citing MB Aminuddin statement; MalaysiaGazette retrospective)
- **17 April 2026**: The Dewan Keadilan dan Undang (DKU) holds a Special Sitting at Istana Besar Seri Menanti, Kuala Pilah, presided over by Tuanku Muhriz. The DKU agrees to advise and accept the consensus of the Telaga Undang, Waris Klana Hulu, Luak Sungei Ujong that Mubarak Dohak's dismissal is valid and final. (Bernama, Malay Mail, FMT, The Sun, Apr 20 2026)
- **19 April 2026 (Sunday)**: Datuk Mubarak Dohak signs and reads a declaration at the Balai Undang Luak Sungai Ujong purporting to depose Yang di-Pertuan Besar Tuanku Muhriz Tuanku Munawir. The declaration is delivered two days after the DKU sitting that confirmed Mubarak no longer holds the office of Undang Luak. (All Apr 20 2026 coverage; The Edge: "Attempt brewing in Negeri Sembilan to remove Ruler — sources")
- **20 April 2026**: MB Datuk Seri Aminuddin Harun issues the official state-government response:
  - The declaration "cannot be accepted and is not recognised."
  - Mubarak "no longer holds any authority or function as the Undang Luak Sungei Ujong."
  - The act is "inconsistent with Articles 10 to 12 of the Undang-Undang Tubuh Kerajaan Negeri Sembilan (UUTKNS) 1959."
  - (BERNAMA, Malay Mail, FMT, The Vibes, The Sun, Scoop, Harian Metro, Berita Harian, Buletin TV3, Malaysiakini — all Apr 20 2026)
- **20 April 2026**: The Telaga Undang (the customary-law body representing the Luak Sungei Ujong royal lineage) publicly labels Mubarak's declaration "menderhaka" (treasonous against the ruler). The Telaga Undang clarifies that Mubarak's removal on 13 May 2025 means he has no customary standing either. (FMT Bahasa; Berita Harian; Buletin TV3)
- **20 April 2026**: A legal source close to the palace, quoted by The Edge, states: **"There is no provision under the Negeri Sembilan State Constitution of 1959 that permits the unilateral removal of the Yang di-Pertuan Besar in the manner asserted."**

## ACTORS

**Directly involved:**
- **Yang di-Pertuan Besar Tuanku Muhriz Tuanku Munawir** — 11th YDPB of Negeri Sembilan (proclaimed 29 Dec 2008). Born 14 Jan 1948. Only son of 9th YDPB Tuanku Munawir. Has presided over ceremonial and constitutional affairs of the state since 2008.
- **Datuk Mubarak Dohak** — Former Undang Luak Sungai Ujong (i.e., former Datuk Klana Petra Luak Sungei Ujong). Dismissed 13 May 2025 on 33 offences under Clause 14(3) UUTKNS. Issued deposal declaration 19 Apr 2026. Historically, the same individual who proclaimed Tuanku Muhriz as 11th YDPB on 29 Dec 2008.
- **MB Datuk Seri Aminuddin Harun** — Menteri Besar of Negeri Sembilan (PKR). State government's official voice rejecting the declaration.

**Customary institutions:**
- **Telaga Undang, Waris Klana Hulu, Luak Sungei Ujong** — The customary-law council that actually voted to dismiss Mubarak (2025). The "owner" of the Undang Luak Sungei Ujong office under Adat Perpatih custom.
- **Dewan Keadilan dan Undang (DKU) Negeri Sembilan** — The state's council of justice and custom that formally accepted Mubarak's dismissal on 17 Apr 2026. Presided over by YDPB Tuanku Muhriz.
- **Undang Yang Empat** — The collective body of 4 Undang Luaks (Sungai Ujong, Jelebu, Johol, Rembau). Each is a Malay Raja in his luak. The 1959 State Constitution makes the YDPB dependent on their approval for most royal discretionary matters.

**Historical precedent:**
- **Datuk Mohammad Jan Abdul Ghani** — 14th Undang Luak Johol, dismissed 27 Mar 2016 for 13 offences under Clause 14(3) UUTKNS. Precedent that Undang Luak *can* be removed by the Telaga Undang + DKU mechanism.

## RELEVANT LAW

1. **Undang-Undang Tubuh Kerajaan Negeri Sembilan (UUTKNS) 1959** — the state constitution of Negeri Sembilan. Lays out the elective-monarchy structure. Enacted when the state joined the Federation of Malaya.
   - **Articles 7 and 11**: Election and proclamation of the YDPB by the Undang Yang Empat from among the eligible princes of the reigning house, per Adat Perpatih.
   - **Articles 10 to 12** (cited by MB as the basis for rejecting Mubarak's declaration): govern the status of the YDPB and the limits on Undang authority. (Specific text requires the gazette — the summary public-facing form is that these articles do not authorise a unilateral deposal by any single Undang, much less a former one.)
   - **Clause 14(3)**: Mechanism by which an Undang Luak can be dismissed for offences against customs and traditions. Invoked in the 2016 Johol precedent and the 2025 Sungei Ujong dismissal.
2. **Federal Constitution of Malaysia**
   - **Article 71**: Federal guarantee of the constitutions and succession rules of each state. State Rulers cannot be unilaterally removed by federal intervention; their removal, if any, must follow the state constitution.
   - **Article 181**: Sovereignty of the Rulers; personal immunity (with narrow exceptions under the Special Court).
3. **Sedition Act 1948**
   - Sections 3(1)(a) and 4 criminalise seditious tendency including acts bringing into hatred or contempt or exciting disaffection against any Ruler. The Telaga Undang's characterisation of Mubarak's declaration as "menderhaka" (treason against the Ruler) places this within the Sedition Act's potential purview, though no formal complaint has been filed publicly as of 20 Apr 2026.
4. **Penal Code**
   - **Sections 121–124**: Offences against the Yang di-Pertuan Agong and Rulers of States. Would apply if criminal proceedings were initiated.
5. **Adat Perpatih** — The matrilineal customary-law system unique to Negeri Sembilan, derived from Minangkabau (West Sumatra) traditions. Lineage traced through the maternal line; leadership roles typically held by men chosen from eligible noble families within each luak. Formalised into the UUTKNS 1959 but retains unwritten customary force alongside.

## KEY STATISTICS

- **1** — Number of Malaysian states with an elective monarchy (Negeri Sembilan is the only one).
- **4** — Number of Undang Luaks (Undang Yang Empat): Sungai Ujong, Jelebu, Johol, Rembau.
- **11th** — Tuanku Muhriz's position in the line of YDPBs of Negeri Sembilan since Dato' Lenggang / pre-British period.
- **~253 years** — Elective-ruler practice in place since approximately 1773 per formal historical records (per Wikipedia / Malay Mail explainer, Apr 20 2026).
- **67 years** — Since UUTKNS 1959 was enacted (1959 → 2026).
- **33** — Offences cited against Mubarak Dohak in his 13 May 2025 dismissal as Datuk Klana Petra.
- **13** — Offences cited against the 14th Undang Luak Johol in his 27 Mar 2016 dismissal (the precedent).
- **~17.3 years** — Tuanku Muhriz's reign so far (29 Dec 2008 → 20 Apr 2026).
- **0** — Known precedents under UUTKNS 1959 for a former Undang Luak deposing a sitting YDPB unilaterally.

1. **Sentiment** — HIGH: Royal matters carry strong affective loading among Malaysians of all communities. Risk of sympathy accumulation toward either the palace or the "rebel" former Undang. Target aggregate near-zero sentiment; frame as constitutional inquiry, not drama.
2. **Political** — MEDIUM-HIGH: Mubarak proclaimed Tuanku Muhriz in 2008, then sought to depose him in 2026. Any coverage must avoid suggesting coalition-politics motivation absent primary-source evidence. Anwar's unity government does not appear to have a direct stake here, but state-federal relations on royal matters are always politically charged.
3. **Ethnic** — LOW: Intra-Malay customary-law dispute. No cross-ethnic dimension.
4. **Religious** — LOW: YDPB has religious headship duties but this dispute is constitutional/customary, not religious-doctrinal.
5. **Narrative** — HIGH: Active/passive voice distribution matters. Do not frame Mubarak as a lone rebellion agent (Telaga Undang, DKU, state government, Federal Constitution all stand against his action). Equally, do not sanctify Tuanku Muhriz by omission — state his continuing power is uncontested by any primary-source legal analysis. The story is that the UUTKNS has a clear mechanism for Undang Luak removal but none for unilateral YDPB removal, and this asymmetry is the constitutional design.
6. **Completeness** — HIGH: Must include the 2008 irony (Mubarak proclaimed the same YDPB), the 2016 Johol precedent (shows what legitimate dismissal looks like), the 2025 Mubarak dismissal (shows why he now has no standing), and the Edge's anonymous legal-source quote on the absence of any deposal provision. Omission of any one of these produces an incomplete picture.
7. **Temporal** — MEDIUM: Fresh story (announcement date 19 Apr 2026). Likely to evolve — possible legal action against Mubarak under Sedition Act, possible civil suit, possible further statements from the palace. Frame as "as of 20 Apr 2026."
8. **Confidence** — HIGH on public facts (dates, actors, statements, legal citations). MEDIUM on customary-law interpretation (Adat Perpatih is partly unwritten). LOW on Mubarak's motivation — no primary-source explanation of his reasons has been issued as of 20 Apr 2026.
9. **Sources** — HIGH: 10+ independent outlets across English/Bahasa (BERNAMA, Malay Mail, FMT, The Vibes, Scoop, Harian Metro, Berita Harian, The Sun, Buletin TV3, Malaysiakini, The Edge) converging on the same core facts + legal primary sources (UUTKNS 1959, NS government portal, Federal Constitution).
10. **Geographic** — LOW: Specific to Negeri Sembilan but carries national-significance implications for how Malaysia handles customary-law-federal-constitution intersections. No Peninsula-vs-East divide.
11. **Economic** — LOW: No direct economic dimension.
12. **Gender** — LOW-MEDIUM: Adat Perpatih is matrilineal (lineage through maternal line) — unique among Malay state customs and a positive gender-system observation. Leadership roles are held by men. No negative gender dimension in this specific dispute but the matrilineal point is worth noting as context.

## CONTRADICTIONS

- **Spelling of Mubarak's surname**: "Dohak" (most English outlets: Malay Mail, BERNAMA, FMT, The Edge, Scoop) vs "Thahak" (MalaysiaGazette, Malaysia Tribune). **Authoritative: "Dohak"** — BERNAMA (national news agency) + MB Aminuddin's statement use "Dohak." We use "Dohak" in copy; note "Thahak" variant only if editorially essential.
- **Date of Mubarak's dismissal**: Most sources say "13 May 2025." MalaysiaGazette mentions the dismissal in a 19 March 2026 retrospective. No contradiction on the 13 May 2025 effective date; the different article-date simply reflects different reporting moments.
- **Mubarak's declared role when reading the deposal**: He signed and read as "Undang Luak Sungai Ujong" — but per DKU ruling (17 Apr 2026) he no longer holds that office. He is a *former* Undang who is *asserting* his authority despite having been removed. Our copy must make this distinction explicit.
- **Timing — 19 April 2026 vs 20 April 2026**: The declaration was read Sunday 19 April. The state-government rejection and broad media coverage came Monday 20 April. Both dates appear in different reports. We use "19 Apr (declaration)" and "20 Apr (state response)."
