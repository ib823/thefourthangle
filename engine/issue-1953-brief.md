# ISSUE BRIEF: 1953

## Headline
Vegetable Prices May Jump 50% in April — The Fertiliser Link

## Context
One-third of global fertiliser shipments transit the Strait of Hormuz. With the waterway disrupted since 28 February 2026, Malaysian farmers face fertiliser costs up 100-150% and are bracing for vegetable price hikes of 30-50% once Hari Raya price controls ended on 28 March. 23 vessels carrying urea, sulphur and phosphates were blocked at Hormuz in early March. China has also curbed fertiliser exports, compounding the shortage.

## Source Date
2026-03-30

## Key Facts
- 23 fertiliser-laden vessels blocked at Hormuz in early March 2026
- One-third of global fertiliser shipments transit Hormuz
- Fertiliser raw material prices surged 100-150% in two weeks
- 25kg bag of nitrogen fertiliser: RM240 → RM305, projected RM350 in April
- Some Malaysian fertiliser suppliers suspended new orders since mid-March
- Cameron Highlands farms rely on imported NPK fertilisers from Germany (NPK 15-15-15, 16-16-16)
- Tomato production costs up 15-20% due to fertigation system disruption
- Leafy vegetables (cabbage, lettuce, sawi) heavily dependent on imported fertiliser
- Cameron Highlands ginger rose RM2/kg in one week
- Farmers warn 30-50% vegetable price hike in April
- Hari Raya price controls on 12 essential vegetables ended 28 March 2026
- Post-controls, accumulated cost pressure passes to consumers immediately
- China curbed fertiliser exports, compounding Hormuz disruption
- Malaysia imports ~60% of its food
- CNBC reported fertiliser disruption as separate from oil story
- Malay Mail published detailed Hormuz-to-kitchen explainer

- FAO data shows Malaysia heavily dependent on imported fertilisers
