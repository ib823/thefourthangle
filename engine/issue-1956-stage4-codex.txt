# ISSUE BRIEF: 1956

## Headline
Expat Salary Floor Doubles to RM20K — The Talent Equation

## Context
From 1 June 2026, Employment Pass Category I minimum salary rises from RM10,000 to RM20,000 per month. Category III capped at 5 years with mandatory succession planning. The policy aims to prioritise local talent under the 13th Malaysia Plan, but critical sectors face talent shortages no domestic pipeline can fill by June.

## Source Date
2026-03-30

## Key Facts
- Revised Employment Pass salary policy effective 1 June 2026
- Cabinet approved 17 October 2025
- Category I: RM10,000 → RM20,000/month minimum (valid up to 10 years)
- Category II: RM10,000-RM19,999/month range
- Category III: RM5,000-RM9,999 (RM7,000 minimum in manufacturing); capped at 5 years
- 10-year max duration tied to employer, not individual
- Succession planning mandatory for EP II and III — structured approach to transition roles to Malaysians
- Aligns with 13th Malaysia Plan (RMK-13) to reduce foreign labour reliance
- Singapore EP minimum: SGD5,000 (~RM16,600) — Malaysia's new Cat I floor exceeds this
- Industries affected: technology, manufacturing, professional services, digital economy, O&G, semiconductors
- Malaysia semiconductor workforce target: 60,000 by 2030
- Current pipeline: ~3,000 relevant graduates per year
- Penang E&E cluster flagged concerns about 5-year cap losing institutional memory
- Knowledge transfer sounds reasonable but 5-year cap affects longer-timeline projects
- Multinationals allocating headcount between Penang and Singapore now face higher Malaysia threshold
- All new and renewal applications from 1 June must comply
- Several law firms (Baker McKenzie, KPMG, Fragomen, Shearman & Sterling) published advisories
- One commentary called it "one of the most consequential labour policy shifts in over a decade"
