# ISSUE BRIEF: 1955

## Headline
No Raya Open Houses — Austerity Worth 0.01% of the Subsidy Bill

## Context
Cabinet banned all government and GLC Hari Raya open houses and restricted ministers' overseas travel as a cost-saving measure amid the Hormuz crisis. The annual cost of official open houses is a fraction of the RM4 billion monthly fuel subsidy bill. States followed suit. The Raya open house is Malaysia's most inclusive political tradition — crossing racial and religious lines.

## Source Date
2026-03-30

## Key Facts
- Cabinet decision announced ~11 March 2026 by PM Anwar Ibrahim
- All ministries, agencies, GLCs and GLICs banned from hosting Hari Raya open houses
- Ministers' overseas travel restricted to pre-scheduled or compulsory engagements
- Framed as fiscal discipline and early precautionary measure
- Anwar: "preferred to take early precautionary measures rather than risk being caught unprepared"
- State governments followed: Negeri Sembilan, Melaka cancelled state-level open houses
- Official Raya open houses typically cost RM2-5 million across all ministries combined (estimated)
- Monthly fuel subsidy bill: RM4 billion (RM48B annualised at current crude)
- Savings represent approximately 0.001% of monthly subsidy expenditure
- Open houses are Malaysia's most cross-racial, cross-religious political tradition
- Backbenchers and state assemblymen use Raya open houses as primary constituent engagement events
- Private citizens and businesses can still host their own open houses
- The ban covers government events only, not private celebrations
- Hari Raya Aidilfitri 2026 falls on 30-31 March
- Decision came alongside WFH announcement and fuel quota cut signals
- GLCs and GLICs received same directive (no open houses)
- Austerity optics: government signalling willingness to restrain at the top before asking public to tighten belts
