# ISSUE BRIEF: 1951

## Headline
Seven Ships, One Phone Call, Zero Leverage

## Context
Seven Malaysian tankers (3 Petronas, 2 MISC, 1 Sapura, 1 Sepadu) were stranded in the Strait of Hormuz after Iran blocked the waterway on 28 February 2026 following US-Israel military strikes. PM Anwar secured passage via a direct call to Iran's President Pezeshkian. Malaysia was NOT on Iran's original 5-nation safe-passage list (China, Russia, India, Iraq, Pakistan). Nearly 2,000 ships from other nations remain blocked. Brent crude peaked at $126/barrel. Maritime traffic through Hormuz dropped 90%.

## Source Date
2026-03-30

## Key Facts
- Crisis began 28 Feb 2026 after US-Israel strikes on Iran (killing supreme leader Khamenei)
- Iran's IRGC declared strait closed, threatening to attack any vessels
- 7 Malaysian vessels stranded: 3 Petronas, 2 MISC, 1 Sapura, 1 Sepadu
- Iran initially allowed only 5 nations: China, Russia, India, Iraq, Pakistan
- Malaysia secured passage after Anwar's direct call to Pezeshkian on 26 March
- FM Mohamad Hasan confirmed 7 ships awaiting "safe window" on 28 March
- ~2,000 ships blocked globally, maritime traffic down 90%
- Brent peaked at $126/barrel on 8 March (highest since 2022)
- 20% of global daily oil supply transits Hormuz
- One-third of global fertiliser shipments transit Hormuz
- Malaysia has no naval assets in the Gulf, no membership in Combined Maritime Forces
- Insurance premiums for Gulf-bound vessels surged significantly
